# Solar Savings Analysis Since GetEnergy Switch

Analysis of actual money saved by the solar panels from the start of GetEnergy electricity service (Jan 23, 2026) through the most recent billed period (May 19, 2026) — 117 days across four billing periods. Companion to [[Solar Panel Return on Investment Calculation]].

**Sources:** GetEnergy consolidated statements Feb–May 2026 and the June 2026 bill (this folder, electricity detail pages); daily generation from the *Actual Solar results* tab of the [Solar Panel Cash Flow Analysis sheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1KxinFPRpDB_1VxSeR8c7TBzisJcn1LvKMDEsbtkMVBU/edit?usp=sharing).

## Bottom line

**The panels have saved approximately $279 (incl. GST) since the switch.** Without solar, the four electricity bills would have totalled roughly **$596**; the actual bills totalled **$316.65** — a 47% reduction, with the final bill landing as a **$41.90 credit**.

Savings including 5% GST: **$279.45**. The trend is the story — savings roughly tripled each period as the days lengthened, then jumped again when the Solar Club 35¢ rate took effect Apr 21.

Total generation over the window was 2,070 kWh, which reconciles exactly with the bills: every period's generation equals exports plus self-consumption (kWh figures cross-checked between the bills' meter detail and the spreadsheet tab).

## How savings were calculated

Savings = (what the bills would have been without solar) − (actual bills). Three components:

1. **Microgen export credits: +$304.43.** Taken directly from the bills' Microgen Credit lines. The Apr 21 switch to the Solar Club fixed 35¢/kWh rate did the heavy lifting — 743 kWh exported in one period earned $260.05.
2. **Self-consumed solar: +$84.28.** 683 kWh used directly in the house never appeared on a bill. Each avoided kWh saves the 6.89¢ energy charge plus volumetric delivery: 3.9¢/kWh transmission and 1.55¢/kWh variable distribution. (Delivery rates derived by fitting the four bills' own transmission and distribution lines; the distribution fit is $0.769/day fixed + $0.0155/kWh, accurate to within a cent on every bill.)
3. **Solar Club import premium: −$122.56.** The 35¢ rate cuts both ways — the 436 kWh imported in Apr 21–May 19 cost $122.56 more than it would have at 6.89¢. Worth it: the rate change still netted +$86 for that period versus staying on the old rate, and the gap widens every month exports exceed imports.

**Key assumption:** the no-solar counterfactual keeps you on the regular fixed 6.89¢/kWh plan (your actual pre–Solar Club rate). Fixed charges (admin, distribution daily charge, transmission riders) are identical in both scenarios and cancel out. The Green Offset Charge (1.85¢/kWh, cancelled May 6) was excluded as roughly cost-neutral to the comparison.

## Context against the loan

Four loan payments (~$735 at $183.75/month) fell in this window, so net cash flow to date is roughly **−$456**. That's expected: this window was entirely winter and spring ramp-up, and only one period enjoyed the 35¢ rate. The current period (May 20 onward) is tracking ~1,162 kWh of generation per the spreadsheet estimate — at that pace the next bill should be a substantially larger credit, and summer periods should individually out-earn the monthly loan payment.

## Watch items

The 35¢ fixed rate is locked only until Apr 30, 2027 (per the June bill's rate line) — the economics reset then. The spreadsheet's annual model projected $550 of savings for Jan–May; actuals came in at $279, but the model's monthly rows were already flagged as inconsistent in [[Solar Panel Return on Investment Calculation]], and the high-earning summer months are still ahead. Revisit after two or three summer bills to recalibrate the annual estimate.
