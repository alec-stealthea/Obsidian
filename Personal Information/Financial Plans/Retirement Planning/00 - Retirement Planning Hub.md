## How this works

You supply the facts; I (acting as your financial advisor) turn them into analysis, scenarios, and recommendations. This hub is the index. Each numbered file below is either an **input** file (you fill in the blanks) or an **analysis** file (I build it once the inputs exist). Fill the input files as completely as you can — partial is fine to start, and flag anything you're unsure of with `??`.

> **Not formal advice.** I'm not a licensed financial planner, accountant, or tax advisor. Everything here is decision-support to help you ask better questions and arrive informed. Confirm pension, tax, and benefit specifics with your pension plan administrator and a fee-for-service planner / accountant before acting.

## The central question

You reach **unreduced pension age in 2030**, but working two more years to **2032** raises your pension income substantially (more pensionable service, higher highest-average salary, two more years of full-salary saving, and a shorter drawdown horizon). Consulting income from Stealth EA is **no longer assumed** in the base plan.

So the plan is built to answer one core question:

> **Does retiring in 2030 vs. working to 2032 leave me better off, and by how much — after tax, after lifestyle, and after accounting for two fewer years of freedom?**

Everything in this folder feeds that comparison.

## Suggested order to fill things in

1. **01 Profile** — sets the timeline and assumptions everything else hangs on.
2. **02 Income Sources** — the single most important input is your **pension estimate at both 2030 and 2032**. Request these from your plan administrator first; they drive the whole analysis.
3. **04 Expenses** — what you actually need to spend determines whether either date "works."
4. **03 Assets** and **05 Debts** — the buffer between income and spending.
5. **09 / 10** — coverage and estate gaps.
6. Once 01–05 are reasonably complete, tell me and I'll build **06, 07, 08, and 11**.
