## Who Alec Is (at a glance)

- **Life purpose:** Creating meaningful individual relationships that contribute to amazing group outcomes benefiting his community. He sees himself as someone who opens "Pandora's Box" — showing people the art of the possible within their own skills journey.
- **Core values:** Availability, awareness, supportiveness, intentionality, enthusiasm, collaboration, empowerment. Early drafts also surfaced: Creating, Sharing, Opening Pandora's Box, Making Epic Mistakes, and "Git Er Done."
- **Aspirational identity (from "Which Homework"):** Chooses to be patient, vibrant ("live life with flair"), and a builder (contributor to society). Chooses to connect with people, exercise five times a week, and set aside time for singing every day.
- **Definition of success:** Kids becoming independent; being recognized for professional expertise; contributing skills to nonprofits; meaningful experiences with wife and family.
- **Definition of failure:** Becoming sedentary and no longer using his time, treasure, and talent for worthwhile causes.
- **What energizes him:** Starting meaningful initiatives, being creative (music, writing, infographics), performing.
- **What drains him:** Administrivia, confrontational situations, "ticky box" activities and death-march initiatives.
- **Fun & interests:** Singing and playing music, watching hockey/rugby, reading broadly, writing, cooking for family and friends.
- **Self-described quirks:** Precise/semantically correct language, British-style humour, taking things seriously.
- **Unique strengths:** Big-picture thinking with a systematic ability to break complex problems into simpler pieces; unusually high focus and productivity.
- **Mantras:** "Family comes first" and "Don't look forward, don't look back, enjoy where you're at."
- **Against:** Bullying.

## Boardroom Personas

From the coaching engagement, Alec identified a set of inner "boardroom" personas — archetypes that represent different voices in his head. Understanding these helps recognize when a particular persona is driving behaviour:

- **Fernando** ("It's better to look good than to be good") — The inner critic/judge. Fears not conforming to norms. Antidote: don't worry about what others think.
- **Wayne** ("You're not worthy") — Impostor syndrome. Fears being found out. Threat: not taking chances.
- **Spock** ("Logically…") — Over-analyzer. Sets impossibly high standards for logical rigour. Threat: over-analyzing situations instead of acting.
- **Ernie** ("You can do it!") — The coach/improver. Looks for ways to build skills and visualize success. Threat: setting unrealistically high expectations.
- **Alexander** ("We're going that way") — The visionary leader. Paints compelling futures and leads from the front. Threat: choosing the wrong moment to lead.
- **Ferris** ("Don't need to do that now") — The procrastinator. Puts things off or prioritizes trivial over important. Threat: not renegotiating commitments.
- **Lacey** ("Live and love in the moment") — Joy and presence. Excited about small things. Threat: not appreciating small moments.
