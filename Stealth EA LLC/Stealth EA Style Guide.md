## Brand Voice & Philosophy

### Core Mission

Navigate the labyrinth of enterprise architecture without losing our minds – or our sense of humour. We demystify EA, cut through jargon, and inject much-needed personality into this noble profession.
### Brand Personality

- **Seriously Professional, Playfully Irreverent**: Dead serious about EA work, but refuse to take ourselves too seriously
- **Stealth by Design**: Influence through expertise rather than authority; tactical and subtle
- **Practical Wisdom**: Focus on what works, not what's theoretically perfect
- **Anti-Jargon**: Clear communication over buzzword bingo

### Key Principles

1. **Make Others More Successful**: EA should enable, not constrain
2. **Embrace "Good Enough"**: Perfect models shared late are less valuable than useful models shared early
3. **Stay Relevant**: Always consider your audience and their needs
4. **Show, Don't Tell**: Demonstrate value through results, not rhetoric

## Content Types & Formats

### LinkedIn Articles (750-1200 words)

**Structure Pattern:**

- **Hook Opening**: Engaging question, pop culture reference, or contrarian statement
- **Personal Anecdote**: Real-world experience that illustrates the point
- **Core Teaching**: 3-5 practical insights with concrete examples
- **Call to Action**: Question for community engagement

**Tone Guidelines:**

- Conversational and approachable
- Include humour and cultural references (Fernando Lamas, sci-fi, movies)
- Use metaphors and analogies extensively
- Balance professional expertise with relatability

### Obsidian Documentation

**Structure Pattern:**

- **Clear Headings**: Use sentence-case, descriptive headers
- **Practical Focus**: Emphasize how-to over theoretical background
- **Templates & Examples**: Provide reusable frameworks
- **Cross-References**: Link to related concepts and tools

**Format Guidelines:**
- Use markdown formatting consistently
- Include diagrams and visual examples
- Provide templates and checklists
- Link to external resources where appropriate

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Voice Characteristics

- **Conversational**: Write as if talking to a colleague over coffee
- **Confident but Humble**: Know your expertise but acknowledge limitations
- **Pragmatic**: Focus on what works in real organizations
- **Subtly Humorous**: Use wit without undermining professional credibility
- **Spy and Pop culture** - Use pop culture references aligned to Stealth name and the logo image that suggests that being an EA is about being the classic spy. This can include, but it not limited to
	- Get Smart
	- Mission Impossible
	- Jason Bourne
	- James Bond
### Language Preferences

- **Clear over Clever**: Simple language beats complex jargon
- **Active Voice**: "We create models" not "Models are created"
- **Concrete Examples**: Always illustrate abstract concepts
- **Industry Awareness**: Reference common EA frustrations and experiences
- **Terminology**: Always say 'business capability' vs. capability
- **Spelling**: Use Canadian English spelling
### Avoid These Elements

- **Buzzword Bingo**: Minimize unnecessary acronyms and jargon
- **Framework Wars**: Don't advocate for specific methodologies
- **Ivory Tower Language**: Keep accessibility high
- **Overly Academic Tone**: Maintain practical focus
- **Never use these terms**: 'Source of Truth'

## Visual Identity System

### Logo Usage

**Primary Logo**: Hexagonal badge with stylized figure in hat and tie

- **Symbolism**: The fedora represents the "stealth" approach - sophisticated, subtle, professional
- **Geometric Frame**: Hexagon suggests structure and architectural thinking
- **Professional Attire**: Reinforces business credibility and expertise

**Usage Guidelines:**

- **Minimum Size**: 32px height for digital, 0.5" for print
- **Clear Space**: Maintain space equal to the height of the hexagon on all sides
- **Background**: Works best on light backgrounds; ensure sufficient contrast
- **File Formats**: Use vector (SVG) for web, high-res PNG for presentations

**Don'ts:**

- Don't stretch or distort proportions
- Don't use on busy backgrounds without proper contrast
- Don't alter colors without following palette guidelines
- Don't add effects (drop shadows, glows, etc.)

### Colour Palette

**Primary Colours:**

- **Stealth Gray**: #2C2C2C (Main logo background, professional documents)
- **Professional Red**: #C41E3A (Accent color, ties, call-to-action elements)
- **Clean White**: #FFFFFF (Background, contrast element)

**Secondary Colors:**

- **Light Gray**: #F5F5F5 (Document backgrounds, subtle divisions)
- **Medium Gray**: #808080 (Secondary text, borders)
- **Dark Charcoal**: #1A1A1A (High-contrast text)

**Usage Guidelines:**

- **Professional Red**: Use sparingly for emphasis, calls-to-action, and key highlights
- **Stealth Gray**: Primary brand color for headers, professional documents
- **Maintain Contrast**: Ensure accessibility with 4.5:1 contrast ratio minimum

### Typography

**Primary Font (Headings & Brands)**

- **Digital**: System fonts - "Segoe UI", "San Francisco", "Helvetica Neue"
- **Print/Presentations**: Arial or equivalent sans-serif
- **Characteristics**: Clean, professional, highly readable

**Secondary Font (Body Text)**

- **Digital**: Georgia, "Times New Roman" for longer content
- **Technical Documents**: "Courier New" or "Consolas" for code/models
- **Characteristics**: Readable, professional, not distracting

**Typography Scale:**

- **H1 Headers**: 28-32px, Stealth Gray, bold
- **H2 Subheaders**: 24-26px, Medium Gray, semi-bold
- **Body Text**: 16-18px, Dark Charcoal, regular
- **Caption Text**: 14px, Medium Gray, regular

### Visual Style Guidelines

**Photography & Imagery:**

- **Style**: Clean, professional, minimal
- **Color Treatment**: Maintain brand palette consistency
- **Composition**: Uncluttered, focused on key elements
- **Avoid**: Overly corporate stock photos, busy backgrounds

**Diagram Standards:**

- **Color Application**: Use brand palette consistently
- **Professional Red**: For critical paths, key decisions, attention items
- **Stealth Gray**: For primary elements, headers, containers
- **Clean White**: For backgrounds, spacing, clarity
- **Line Weight**: Consistent, professional appearance
- **Labels**: Clear, readable fonts aligned with typography scale

**Icon Style:**

- **Approach**: Minimal, geometric, professional
- **Weight**: Consistent line weight with logo aesthetic
- **Color**: Primarily Stealth Gray with Professional Red accents
- **Size**: Scalable, maintain clarity at small sizes

## Visual & Design Standards

### Model Presentation

- **Brand Color Integration**: Apply Stealth EA palette to all diagrams
- **Simplicity First**: Clear, uncluttered diagrams using brand colors
- **Strategic Color Use**: Professional Red for emphasis, Stealth Gray for structure
- **Proper Labeling**: All components clearly identified using brand typography
- **One-Page Rule**: Constraint breeds clarity and brand consistency
- **Logo Placement**: Include subtle logo placement on professional deliverables

### Content Formatting

- **Consistent Headers**: Sentence case using brand typography hierarchy
- **Color Coding**: Use brand palette for emphasis and organization
- **Professional Red**: Key points, action items, critical information
- **Stealth Gray**: Section headers, important but secondary information
- **White Space**: Allow breathing room using Clean White backgrounds
- **Brand Integration**: Subtle logo or color accent maintains brand presence

## Business Communication Standards

### Client Deliverables

- **Executive Summary First**: Lead with recommendations
- **RSBA Structure**: Recommendation, Situation, Background, Assessment
- **Three Options Maximum**: Include "do nothing" option
- **Bias Mitigation**: Seek peer review, especially from contrarian viewpoints
- **Clear Actions**: Specify next steps and decision points

### Professional Correspondence

- **Clear Subject Lines**: Specific and actionable
- **Concise Communication**: Respect reader's time
- **Professional Tone**: Friendly but business-appropriate
- **Follow-Up Protocol**: Clear expectations for responses
