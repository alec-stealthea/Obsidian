# Domain Architect Interview Guide

The following guide is intended to be a common script for the interview panel for a Domain Architect. 

- **Getting to the Interview Candidates -** the following steps hold true for the assessment of resumes for the purpose of the short-list interview as well as the actual interview itself.
- **Interview Panel** - The interview panel should consist of the: hiring manager, Portfolio Enterprise Architect, a CoE Architect, service owner or project manager for the affected scope of the role.  Other people like possible peers in the role or supporting roles like Business Analyst may be appropriate too.
- **Interview Script and Questions** - The following script is provided as a support, but other questions can be added as part of the assessment.
- **Interview Scoring Sheet** - Where the candidate being interviewed is a contractor rather than a full time employee, it is recommended that the linked scoring sheet be completed.  Note that the skills requirements for contracted resources must be from the published Job Posting and need to be split between mandatory and desirable.  It is good practice to do the same for employees in order to have an audit trail.

## Interview Approach

The normal interview is sixty minutes in length with a 5-10 minute introduction, 40-45 minutes of assessment questions and some time at the end for the candidate to ask some questions.  The Interview panel should have made a preliminary assessment of the candidate based on a review of their resume.  Typically, the interview causes the reviewer to change those scores up or down based on the candidates responses.  If possible, it may make sense to ask the candidate to supply a deliverable they can walk through.  For external candidates there may be a confidentially issue that would prevent that from occurring.

## Introduction

- Provide a brief overview of the organization
- Provide a brief overview of the Enterprise Architecture organization
- Provide a brief overview of the way IT is structured
- Review where this role fits within that structure along with scope of responsibilities.

The following behavioral assessment questions are phrased in such a way to allow the applicant to provide real-life narrative to demonstrate that they have done and how they did it.  Each of the question categories has a suggestion of if the skills is mandatory or not as well as the degree of proficiency we expect from the candidate.

## Initial Questions

- Please take 5 minutes to tell us about yourself.
- What interests you about this position?
- Do you have any experience working the healthcare industry.  If not, can you tell us what you understand about the healthcare industry.

## Architect Skills Assessment

|   |   |   |
|---|---|---|
|**Skill**|**Question**|**Guide to assess responses**|
|**Solution Architecture** (mandatory and mastered)|- Please describe how you might review a Solution Architect's overall design and assess the quality of the work?<br>- What experience do you have in developing solutions?|- Does the candidate know how the solution gets broken down?<br>- Can they describe an approach they would use to test the design and overall viability of the solution?<br>- Do they understand that architecture work products support the other roles on the team including the business sponsor, project manager, service owner, business analyst, programmer, etc.?|
|**Systems Design** (desirable and mastered)|- Have you experience working with platform solutions that have a large scope?  What do you find is the biggest difference when compared to smaller applications?|- Can they articulate the difference between the complexity of a large application like Oracle eBusiness Suite or Meditech and other smaller applications?|
|**Business Modelling** (Mandatory and mastered)|- Stakeholder concerns and requirements are key to understanding a business.  Please describe ways you have captured stakeholder requirements.<br>- Please provide an example of how you have modelled the business in the development of a Solution.|- Do they describe a structured approach to understanding requirements from either the user or business sponsor that are above and beyond technical requirements?<br>- Any combination of using a capability model to document structure or business process modelling for dynamics.|
|**Information Systems Coordination**<br><br>(Desirable and Applied)|- Applications do not sit in a vacuum.  What approaches do you have to managing a portfolio of applications?|- Would ideally like to see the way that business architecture and capabilities are linked to applications.<br>- Any demonstration of application lifecycle management like the TIME (Tolerate, Invest, Migrate, and Eliminate) model AHS is using.|
|**Enterprise and Business Architecture Development** (Desirable and applied)|- Describe how you have applied Enterprise Architecture practices and standards in developing solutions?<br>- Are you familiar with any of the leading Enterprise Architecture frameworks?  If so, which ones and what do you know about them?<br>- What experience do you have with developing business aligned IT roadmaps ?|- Having TOGAF certification or the ability to clearly express concepts of Enterprise Architecture.<br>- Understanding how to apply organizational standards in the process of solution design.<br>- Candidate frameworks include Pragmatic EA, Zachman and TOGAF.<br>- Do they understand the complexity of adding time to stage architecture changes.|

## Soft Skills Assessment

|   |   |   |
|---|---|---|
|**Communications**<br><br>(Mandatory and Mastered)|- Domain Architects need to be able to communicate ideas clearly to many different audiences.  Describe the way you approach communications for the stakeholders in a particular solution.|- Demonstrate an understanding that communications is multi-faceted using many different mediums.<br>- The interview itself will indicate how well the candidate can clearly verbalize ideas and concepts.|
|**Facilitation**<br><br>(Desirable, Applied)|- Eliciting requirements and understanding design alternatives usually means dealing with groups of stakeholders.  Describe a session where you facilitated a group session and what you did to get the information you required.|- Demonstration of one of many facilitation techniques including: mind maps, solution modelling from a blank canvas, brainstorming using sticky notes, etc.<br>- Showing how to get non-contributors involved.<br>- Demonstrating the power of clarifying questions.|
|**Leadership Through Influence**<br><br>(Mandatory, Applied)|- Architects have little authority in the lifecycle of the solution process.  Describe how you have approached influencing stakeholders through the design process for an unpopular, but appropriate design alternative.|- The ability to perform a systematic [Architecture Alternatives](https://albertahealthservices.sharepoint.com/teams/Architecture/SitePages/SBAR.aspx) assessment.<br>- Understands the need to identify and socialize ideas with appropriate stakeholders.<br>- An example where they were successful in getting their alternative accepted.|
|**Multi-disciplinary Teams**<br><br>(Desirable, Applied)|- The architect is one role of the multidisciplinary project team.  Can you provide examples of how you work with a couple of roles within a team?<br><br>- Project Manager<br>- Developer<br>- Business Analyst|- Looking for an awareness of the difference between roles.<br>- Demonstration of the different things a particular role requires from the architect.|
