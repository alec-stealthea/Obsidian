IgA This antibody plays an important role in the body's defenses against the
invasion of microorganisms through mucous membrane-lined surfaces,
including the nose, eyes, lungs, and intestines. IgA is found in the blood
and in secretions such as those in the GI tract and in the nose, eyes,
lung, and breast milk.
The most prevalent type of antibody, it is produced upon subsequent
IgG exposure to an antigen. The secondary antibody response is faster and
more abundant than the primary antibody response. IgG is present in
both the blood and the tissue. It is the only antibody that is transferred
across the placenta from the mother to fetus. The mohter's IgG
protects the fetus and newborn until the infant's immune system can
produce its own antibodies.
IgM This antibody that is produced upon initial exposure to an antigen. IgM
is abundant in the blood but is not normally present in organs or tissues.

Polymerase Chain Reaction PCR is a highly sensitive test that can detect small amounts of DNA or
