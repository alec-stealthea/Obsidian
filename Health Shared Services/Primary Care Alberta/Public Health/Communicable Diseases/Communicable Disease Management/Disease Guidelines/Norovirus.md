## Case Definition
## Confirmed Case
Clinical illness(A) with laboratory confirmation(B) of infection:
- Detection of norovirus nucleic acid (e.g., PCR) in stool
## Probable Case (Outbreak Only)
Clinical illness(A) in a person who is epidemiologically linked to a confirmed norovirus case
Confirmed Outbreak
Two or more cases of clinical illness(A) compatible with norovirus that can be epidemiologically linked to one another
(i.e., associated by exposure with onsets within a one to three day period), at least one of which is laboratory-confirmed:
Community Outbreak
- Two or more unrelated(C) cases of illness compatible with norovirus that can be epidemiologically linked to one another
Institutional Outbreak
- Two or more cases of illness compatible with norovirus that are epidemiologically linked in an institutional setting
(A) Clinical illness is characterized by acute onset of nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, myalgia, headache, malaise, low grade fever
or a combination of these symptoms, lasting 24–48 hours.
(B) In general, laboratory testing is only performed in relation to gastrointestinal illness outbreak investigations.
(C) Do not live in a common household, excluding institutions.

## Reporting Requirements
Laboratories
All laboratories shall report all positive laboratory results in the prescribed form by mail, fax or electronic transfer within 48
hours (two business days) to the:
- Chief Medical Officer of Health (or designate), and
- Medical Officer of Health (or designate) of the zone.
Alberta Health Services and First Nations and Inuit Health Branch
- Report outbreaks only using the Alberta Outbreak Report Form.
- Completion of a Notifiable Disease Report is not required.
