## Case Definition
## Confirmed Case
Clinical illness(A) with laboratory confirmation of infection:
- Isolation of  105 Bacillus cereus organisms per gram(B) from the stool or epidemiologically implicated food.(C)
## Probable Case (Outbreak Only)
Clinical illness(A) in a person who is epidemiologically linked to a confirmed case.
(A) Clinical illness is characterized in some cases by the sudden onset of nausea and vomiting, and in others by abdominal cramps and
diarrhea.
(B) Isolation of B.cereus in numbers less the 105 organisms per gram may be indicative of normal flora only and not necessarily the causative
agent.
(C) Refer to the Provincial Laboratory for Public Health (ProvLab) Guide to Services for current specimen collection and submission
information.

## Reporting Requirements
Laboratories
All laboratories shall report all positive laboratory results by mail, fax or electronic transfer within 48 hours (two business days)
to the:
- Chief Medical Officer of Health (or designate), and
- Medical Officer of Health (or designate) of the zone.
Alberta Health Services and First Nations and Inuit Health Branch
- Report outbreaks only using the Alberta Outbreak Report Form.
- Completion of a Notifiable Disease Report is not required.
