## Case Definition(1)
NOTE: Alberta Health will update this guideline as new information becomes available.
## Probable Case
A person ≤ 16 years of age who presents with severe acute hepatitis since October 1, 2021 and requiring hospitalization,
AND
- With serum transaminase >500 IU/L (AST or ALT)
AND
- Excluding hepatitis caused or attributed to a hepatitis virus (A, B, C, D, E)(A) or known or expected presentation of a drug or
medication; a genetic congenital, or metabolic condition; an oncologic, vascular, or ischemia related condition; or an acute
worsening of chronic hepatitis.
(A) If hepatitis D or E serology results are pending or test was not done, but other criteria met, these can be reported as probable cases.

## Reporting Requirements
Physicians
- Alberta physicians(B) shall notify the Medical Officer of Health (MOH) in their zone of all probable cases within 48 hours of
## diagnosis (by phone, fax or email) and include:
Name;
Age;
Date of birth;
Gender;
Personal health number;
## Laboratory report; and
Other relevant clinical/epidemiological information.
- Alberta physicians(B) shall submit the Acute Severe Hepatitis of Unknown Origin in Children Case Report Form to the MOH
within 7 days of initial diagnosis.
Alberta Health Services and First Nations and Inuit Health Branch
- The MOH/designate of the Zone where the case currently resides shall report all probable cases to the CMOH/designate
within 48 hours by email and will include
Name;
Age;
Date of birth;
Gender;
Personal health number;
## Laboratory report; and
Other relevant clinical/epidemiological information.
- The MOH/designate, shall submit the Acute Severe Hepatitis of Unknown Origin in Children Case Report Form by email or
through another mutually agreed upon reporting system (e.g., CDOM) within 7 days of initial notification. Follow up should
be done to determine the evolution of the case’s clinical status and the final report shall be submitted within two weeks of
discharge from hospital or resolution of the case status, whichever occurs first. Report under Rare or Emerging
Communicable Disease.
- For out-of-province and out-of-country reports, the following information should be forwarded to the CMOH (or designate)
within 48 hours of diagnosis and include:
Name;
Age;
Date of birth;
Gender;
Personal health number;
## Laboratory report; and
Other relevant clinical/epidemiological information.
(B) It is expected that this reporting would typically be done by a pediatric specialist in infectious diseases or hepatology following their
## investigations.
