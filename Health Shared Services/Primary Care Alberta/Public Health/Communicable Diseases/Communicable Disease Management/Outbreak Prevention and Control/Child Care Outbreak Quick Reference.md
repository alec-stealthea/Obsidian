# Child Care Outbreak Quick Reference

Child Care Outbreak Checklist
Manage an outbreak
All staff share responsibility to keep children safe. This checklist provides general measures for staff to
complete during an outbreak.
- Follow all recommendations made by the AHS Public Health Outbreak team.
- Refer to the Guide for Outbreak Prevention & Control in Child Care Facilities for more detailed
recommendations and follow facility-specific policies.
Communicate about the outbreak
 Inform parents/guardians, staff and visitors of the outbreak.
 Report children and staff who are newly symptomatic daily to the AHS Public Health Outbreak team.
Use infection prevention control measures
 Encourage children and staff to practice frequent hand hygiene and respiratory etiquette.
 Provide easy access to hand washing stations.
Keep the facility illness free
 Remind parents/guardians to keep children who are ill home.
 Send staff who are ill home.
 Keep children away from others if they are ill at the facility and contact parents/guardians to pick
them up as soon as possible.
Care for children who develop viral respiratory illness
 Offer a mask to children over two years of age if tolerated.
 Have staff wear a mask when caring for a child who is ill.
Care for children who develop GI illness
 Dedicate a washroom for children who are ill.
 Have staff wear gloves and gown/protective clothing while caring for a child who is ill.

Enhance cleaning and disinfection
 Increase the frequency of cleaning and disinfection of common areas and high touch surfaces such
as doorknobs and washrooms.
 Clean and disinfect shared items between users.
 Remove shared items such as craft supplies, books and puzzles that cannot be cleaned and
disinfected.
 Increase the frequency of cleaning and disinfection of shared transport vehicles.
Decrease the use of common or shared items
 Use shared play structures one group at a time.
 Close shared sensory tables including water/sand tables.
Modify activities and food services
 Keep children and staff within their assigned child care room, including during naps, meals and snack
times.
 Postpone high-risk field trips and activities.
 Cease family-style meal service.
 Hand out snacks directly to children.
