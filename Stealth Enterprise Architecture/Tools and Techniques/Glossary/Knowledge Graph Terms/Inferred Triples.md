Inferred triples are those triples that derive from an inference rule rather than those that come from a priori assertions from external sources. Often, such inferred triples provide a convenient index for more complex relationships. For instance, a company may have multiple CEOs over the course of its existence, but only one of these will be the CEO at the current time. An inference rule could be written that look at all employees with the role of CEO, order these by the reversed time that they began their tenure, then create a new triple that removes the singleton "has CEO" property and creates a new one based on the newer date. This not only keeps the information up to date automatically, but also asserts a simple relationship that represents a much more complex one.
