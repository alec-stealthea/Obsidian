**Thesis** - Much of the work being done by governments using regulators is by creating holistic end user journey maps that paint an optimistic path of a consumer from one end to another. The successful implemention of solutions that improve overall ecosystems is quite rare. A key missing link is understanding the business architecture for the value stream network inside and between the players affected within the ecosystem. This means analysing the value streams within each of the players in the ecosystem and the business capabilities that much be implemented with the least amount of standards as possible.

Let's take for example Sure Scripts. This is a success story that is largely is unheralded as a key mechanism that can improve health outcomes. (add summary of Sure Scripts history as a merger between two initiatives and paid for by the health insurance industry)

The value stream network ecosystem stakeholders

1. **Patient** - Patient usually has insurance company that will pay for some or all of the costs of a prescription. The goal for the patient will be to be able to fulfill the prescription with the least out of pocket costs both upfront and overall.
2. **Prescriber** - Usually a primary care provider for the majority.
3. **Insurance company** - looking to serve their interests.
4. **Insurance company payor** - In healthcare the person paying the premiums to the insurance company is rarely the patient themselves directly. It is usually the patient's employer or the government.
5. **Pharmacy** - The last step in the overall patient journey, though for chronic conditions it is more of a closed look where the primary care provider needs to know that the patient has both filfilled the prescription, bu alsp that they refill the prescription to know that they are adhering to the care plan.
