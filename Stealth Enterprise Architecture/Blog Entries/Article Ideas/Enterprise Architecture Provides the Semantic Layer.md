There is a lot of discussion about ontologies and the context layer being important, but little conversation on how to create that layer.  For that I am going to suggest we need Enterprise Architecture to be able to build out ontologies that provide the business context.

A key to the success is taking advantage of open standards where available. The Stealth EA would like to propose that the Open Group ArchiMate Specification provides a reasonable starting point of an ontology for any organization.  Augmenting that ontology with a metamodel that illustrates how your particular organization is going to use ArchiMate in your models adapts the ontology as needed.  From



Domain Driven Design provides a basis to start with a portion of the enterprise.  For the example of the Stealth EA pizzeria, that might be the kitchen where we have a vocabulary of ingredients, recipes, orders, etc.  For the front of house, that may be reservations, comps, etc.

With your domain vocabulary in place, now is the time to build out the domain value streams and value stream networks.  This gets you the why of what needs to be done vs. The what.  Take that down another level to business capabilities to get the what the that value stream needs - at a conceptual level.

Take those business capabilities to the instance level and now you get to the logical level of detail. This is where the business objects get mapped to information objects that are persisted with the domain application portfolio.
