# FinOps and Enterprise Architecture

> **Impetus:** Tokenomics — as AI token-based pricing becomes a primary cost driver, FinOps disciplines are converging with enterprise architecture decisions.

## Research Resources

### 1. FinOps Foundation — Token Economics: The Atomic Unit of AI Value
**URL:** https://www.finops.org/insights/token-economics-the-atomic-unit-of-ai-value/
**Why:** Directly bridges FinOps and tokenomics. Frames token economics as "FinOps applied to AI" — governs variable cost of intelligence computation the same way FinOps governs cloud infrastructure. Key starting point for the blog angle.

---

### 2. Deloitte — Follow the AI Tokens: How CTOs Can Manage Tokenomics
**URL:** https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/services/consulting/articles/future-of-enterprise-it-tokenomics-insights-for-cto.html
**Why:** Enterprise architecture lens. Covers the strategic decision of when to self-host vs. consume AI as a service — "one of the most important financial decisions in enterprise AI." Useful for the EA angle.

---

### 3. Linux Foundation — Tokenomics Foundation Announcement
**URL:** https://www.linuxfoundation.org/press/linux-foundation-announces-the-intent-to-launch-the-tokenomics-foundation-to-establish-open-standards-for-ai-cost-management
**Why:** Signals that open standards for AI cost management are emerging. Context for why this topic matters now and where governance is headed. Good for situating the blog post in the current landscape.

---

### 4. FinOps Foundation — FinOps for AI Overview
**URL:** https://www.finops.org/wg/finops-for-ai-overview/
**Why:** Working group output from the FinOps Foundation specifically on AI cost management practices. Covers visibility, attribution, and financial accountability for AI workloads — directly applicable to EA governance work.

---

### 5. *Cloud FinOps* (O'Reilly) — Storment & Fuller
**URL:** https://www.finops.org/community/finops-book/
**Why:** The foundational practitioner book on FinOps. Provides the conceptual baseline (unit economics, shared responsibility, maturity model) needed before mapping FinOps disciplines onto AI/tokenomics. Second edition available.
