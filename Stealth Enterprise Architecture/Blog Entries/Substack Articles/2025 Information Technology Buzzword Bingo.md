## The Origin Story
Many years ago, I attended a technology conference with colleagues. Before the keynote, someone distributed Buzzword Bingo cards at breakfast. Our mission, should we choose to accept it: spread throughout the auditorium and stand up shouting "BINGO!" when we completed a line.

I was secretly relieved that my card wasn't close. The social mortification of being _that person_ in a crowd of hundreds felt worse than any mission briefing gone wrong. But sure enough, not fifteen minutes into the keynote, one of my colleagues achieved victory. The speaker paused. The crowd murmured. A legend was born.

After a few decades in information technology, I've survived countless hype cycles. Technologies rise, plateau, and either become invisible infrastructure or cautionary tales in architecture review boards. In that spirit, I present the 2025 edition of Technology Buzzword Bingo — updated for our AI-saturated, quantum-curious, zero-trust-obsessed times.
## The 2025 Buzzword Roster

This year's collection reflects where enterprise technology conversations have landed. Some terms survived from last year (looking at you, "Digital Twins"), while others have been retired to the great technology graveyard alongside "Web 2.0" and "Information Superhighway."
### The AI Dominion

The artificial intelligence terms have multiplied like tribbles on the Enterprise:

- **Agentic AI** — The buzzword of the moment. AI systems that autonomously plan and execute tasks without constant human hand-holding. Gartner predicts 15% of routine work decisions will be made by AI agents by 2028.
- **Generative AI (GenAI)** — Still going strong after its ChatGPT-fuelled debut. Now the default prefix for approximately everything.
- **AI Copilots** — Microsoft's branding genius has made this the standard term for AI assistants embedded in productivity tools.
- **Large Language Models (LLM)** — The foundation models powering the generative revolution.
- **Small Language Models (SLM)** — The counter-trend: efficient, task-specific models that run on edge devices.
- **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)** — How enterprises ground their LLMs in actual company data instead of hallucinated confidence.
- **AI Governance** — The grown-up response to "we deployed AI everywhere, now what?"
- **AI TRiSM** — Gartner's framework for AI Trust, Risk, and Security Management. Points for the acronym.
- **Multimodal AI** — AI that processes text, images, audio, and video. Because why limit yourself?
### Security & Architecture

- **Zero Trust** — Assume breach, verify everything. No longer optional.
- **Cybersecurity Mesh** — Distributed, composable security architecture. Sounds impressive in proposals.
- **Post-Quantum Cryptography** — Preparing for the day quantum computers break current encryption.
- **DevSecOps** — Security baked into development pipelines, not bolted on afterwards.
### Computing Paradigms

- **Edge AI** — Processing intelligence at the network edge instead of round-tripping to the cloud.
- **Quantum Computing** — Still mostly theoretical for enterprises, but the buzzword persists.
- **Spatial Computing** — The rebrand of AR/VR/XR. Apple's Vision Pro gave this one new legs.
- **Neuromorphic Computing** — Computing architectures inspired by the human brain. Very niche, very buzzy.
### Enterprise Patterns

- **Digital Twins** — Virtual replicas of physical systems. Market projected to hit $73.5 billion by 2027.
- **Platform Engineering** — Building internal developer platforms. The response to "DevOps is everyone's job" becoming "DevOps is no one's job."
- **Hyperautomation** — Automating everything that can be automated, then automating the automation.
- **Low-Code/No-Code** — Citizen development platforms. Still relevant, still controversial among developers.
- **Composable Architecture** — Building systems from interchangeable business capabilities. (I approve of this one.)
- **Data Fabric** — Unified data management across distributed environments.
- **Federated Learning** — Training AI models across decentralised data without moving the data. Privacy-friendly.
### Operational Concerns

- **AIOps** — AI for IT operations. Automating incident response and infrastructure management.
- **FinOps** — Financial operations for cloud. Because those AWS bills got out of hand.
- **Sustainable IT** — Green computing, energy-efficient data centres. The environmental conscience of the industry.
### The Futurist Corner

- **Autonomous Systems** — Systems that operate independently of human intervention.
- **Machine Customers (Custobots)** — AI agents that purchase goods and services autonomously. Gartner's term.
- **Human Augmentation** — Technology enhancing human cognitive or physical capabilities. Wearables and beyond.

## The Meta-Observation

There's a certain irony in a brand built on "Anti-Jargon: Clear communication over buzzword bingo" publishing an actual buzzword bingo game. But that's precisely the point. These terms exist because they represent genuine technological shifts — the problem isn't the concepts, it's when we use the words as substitutes for understanding.
