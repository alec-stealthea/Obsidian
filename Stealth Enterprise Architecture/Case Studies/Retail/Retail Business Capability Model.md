## Retail Business Capability Map

Retail Business Capability Map

## Retail Customer Experience

### Customer Attraction

#### Appointment Management

The ability to schedule, reschedule, and cancel time-based commitments between the organization and customers, employees, or other parties, optimizing resource utilization and stakeholder convenience.

#### Clienteling

The ability to build and maintain personalized relationships with customers by capturing preferences, purchase history, and interaction patterns to deliver tailored service experiences.

#### Inquiry Management

The ability to receive, track, route, and resolve questions and information requests from customers, employees, or other stakeholders across multiple communication channels.

#### Lead Management

The ability to identify, capture, qualify, nurture, and convert prospective customers or opportunities through the sales or engagement pipeline.

#### Product Information Management

The ability to create, maintain, enrich, and distribute accurate and consistent information about products or services across all channels and touchpoints.

### Service Delivery

#### Inventory Lookup

The ability to query and display the availability, location, and status of goods or resources in real-time across storage locations and channels.

#### Order Management

The ability for authorized person to direct or coordinate the provision of services through a series of authorized service requests.

#### Returns Management

The ability to receive, process, evaluate, and resolve requests to return products or reverse service transactions, including determining disposition and issuing appropriate credits or exchanges.

#### Markdown Management

The ability to plan, execute, and track price reductions on products or services based on lifecycle stage, demand patterns, inventory levels, or promotional strategies.

#### Order Fulfillment

The ability to pick, pack, and prepare orders for delivery or customer collection, coordinating resources to meet service commitments.

#### Transaction Processing

The ability to capture, validate, authorize, and complete the exchange of value between parties, including payment acceptance and receipt generation.

### Customer Relationship

#### Customer Engagement

The ability to initiate, maintain, and deepen interactions with customers through targeted communications, events, and experiences that build loyalty and advocacy.

#### Feedback Management

The ability to solicit, capture, analyze, and act upon opinions, ratings, and suggestions from customers, employees, or other stakeholders to drive continuous improvement.

#### Loyalty Program Management

The ability to design, administer, and optimize programs that reward customers for repeat engagement, purchases, or behaviours that benefit the organization.

#### Preference Management

The ability to capture, store, and apply individual stakeholder choices regarding communication methods, product attributes, service options, and privacy settings.

## Retail Operations

### Assets

The ability to deploy, operate, maintain, upgrade, and dispose of property owned by the organization and considered to have significant value, in a cost-effective way.

#### Equipment Management

The ability to select, acquire, and use all of the items necessary to deliver services.

#### Environment Management

The ability to maintain the physical conditions and support services of facilities, including cleaning, climate control, and amenities, to enable safe and effective service delivery.

#### Facilities Management

The ability to plan for, acquire, assure the operation of , maintenance of, and security of facilities at which care is delivered or other work performed by employees.

### Human Resources

#### Education Management

The ability to develop, deliver, track, and certify clinical training programs and educational content for healthcare professionals, including mandatory certifications, continuing education requirements, competency assessments, and specialized clinical skills development to ensure safe and effective patient care.

#### Employee Relations

The ability to manage all aspects of the relationship between employees and the organization, including improving engagement as well as dealing constructively with workplace issues.

#### Compensation Management

The ability to provide a person with money or other economic value in exchange for goods, labor, or to provide for costs incurred due to injury or travel, for example.

#### Learning Management

The ability to design, deliver, track, and assess educational programs and training activities for employees, customers, or other stakeholders, including compliance training, professional development, and certification programs, while managing learning resources and measuring effectiveness.

#### Recruitment

The ability to identify, attract, evaluate, and select qualified candidates for positions within the organization, while ensuring compliance with applicable regulations and organizational diversity and inclusion objectives.

#### Staff Scheduling

The ability to create, manage, and optimize work shift assignments for healthcare personnel across departments and care units, ensuring adequate coverage while balancing staff preferences, competency requirements, regulatory mandates, and operational efficiency.

#### Talent Management

Activities and efforts by an employee and his or her employer to increase existing job-relevant skills and knowledge.

#### Time Keeping

The ability to accurately track, record, and manage employee work hours, attendance, and time-off for healthcare staff, ensuring compliance with labor regulations, union agreements, and healthcare staffing requirements while supporting payroll processing and workforce analytics.

### Risk

#### Business Continuity Management

The ability to identify risks, threats, and vulnerabilities that could affect an organization's continued operations and provide a framework for building organizational resilience and the capability for an effective response.

#### Emergency / Disaster Management

The ability to create and execute plans to reduce an organization's vulnerability to hazards and to improve the ability to cope with disasters.

#### Incident Management

The ability to identify, respond to, investigate, and resolve adverse events, safety incidents, security breaches, and operational disruptions within the healthcare environment, including root cause analysis, corrective actions, and preventive measures to ensure patient safety and organizational resilience.

#### Loss Prevention

The ability to identify, assess, and mitigate risks of theft, fraud, damage, or shrinkage of organizational assets through policies, procedures, and controls.

#### Quality Assurance

The ability to achieve and maintain a desired level of performance through planning, monitoring, control, and continual improvement of products, services, and processes.

#### Risk Management

The ability to identify, assess, and prioritize risks, and then apply resources to minimize, monitor, and control the probability and impact of adverse events, or to maximize the realization of opportunities.

### Capacity

#### Service Management

The ability to coordinate and oversee the delivery of services to ensure they meet the needs of the organization and its stakeholders, including managing service quality, availability, and performance to optimize operational efficiency and support business objectives.

#### Waitlist Management

The ability to manage the order in which health care services can be scheduled for individual patients, ordinarily based on urgency, availability of resources, and order of request.

### Retail Compliance

#### Information Privacy Management

The ability to determine what information can be shared with a third party.

#### Legal & Regulatory Compliance

The ability to assure and demonstrate conformance to all laws and regulations applicable to the health system.

### Information Technology

#### Application Management

The ability to oversee the complete lifecycle of software applications within the healthcare organization, including application portfolio planning, deployment, maintenance, integration, security, performance optimization, and retirement, ensuring applications support clinical and operational workflows while maintaining compliance with healthcare regulations.

#### Information Exchange

The ability to transmit, receive, and synchronize information between systems, applications, organizations, and external entities through various integration patterns including application programming interfaces, batch file transfers, message queues, and manual workflows.

#### Technology Management

The ability to strategically plan, implement, and govern enterprise technology infrastructure and emerging technologies to support healthcare delivery, including technology assessment, architecture planning, vendor management, technology roadmapping, and ensuring technology investments align with organizational objectives and regulatory requirements.

### Retail Security

#### Fraud Detection

The ability to identify, assess, and prevent fraudulent activities by monitoring, analyzing, and responding to suspicious transactions or behaviours across systems, channels, or processes.

#### Identity Management

The ability to ascertain a distinct, unique identity for a person, as expressed by an identifier that is unique within a certain scope, given characteristics about that individual such as his or her name, date of birth, and gender.

### Finance

#### Accounts Payable

The ability to leverage a form of credit extended by suppliers, employees, and patients, by allowing payment to follow the receipt of products or services.

#### Accounts Receivable

The ability for the organization to issue a legally-enforceable claim for payment from a customer, client, or patient.

#### Cash Management

The ability to collect, handle, and use cash, to forecast and manage cash flow, and to optimize the use of cash.

#### Financial Accounting

The ability to record, classify, summarize, and report financial transactions in accordance with applicable accounting standards and regulatory requirements.

#### Revenue Management

The ability to ensure that debts or obligations are remitted, including those resulting from non-delivery of services or goods under contract.

#### Tax Management

The ability to calculate, report, remit, and optimize tax obligations across all applicable jurisdictions while maintaining compliance with tax laws and regulations.

### Knowledge

#### Analytics

Ability to use population health and operational data, statistical and qualitative analysis, and explanatory and predictive modeling to drive health care policy and health care system decision making.

#### Content Managemnent

The ability to create, curate, review, approve, publish, and retire structured and unstructured content assets, including policies, procedures, marketing materials, and digital content, while maintaining version control, ensuring compliance, and supporting multi-channel distribution.

#### Collaboration

#### Document Management

The ability to store, manage, and track electronic documents and images of paper-based information.

#### Information Management

#### Records Management

The capability to systematically control the creation, distribution, use, maintenance, and disposition of the legal health record, i.e., recorded information maintained as evidence of health care activities and transactions.

#### Master Data Management

The ability to create, manage, and distribute the information pertaining to business entities (e.g., providers, patients, care delivery sites, and services) that are agreed upon and shared across the health system; as well as reference data (value sets, vocabularies, terminologies) that are shared -- nationally or internationally -- among health systems.

### Retail Supply Chain

#### Contract Management

The abilty to negotiate, monitor, renew, and end contracts with vendors and suppliers, including contracted health care providers, acting on behalf of the organization and patient interests when addressing contract terms, product cost, quality, and safety.

#### Demand Planning

The ability to forecast future requirements for products, services, or resources based on historical patterns, market intelligence, and business plans to optimize supply and capacity decisions.

#### Inventory Management

The ability to oversee and control the receipt, storage, movement, and use of goods and materials required for the delivery of products or services.

#### Procurement

The ability to identify needs, source suppliers, negotiate terms, and acquire goods or services required for organizational operations.

#### Recall Management

The ability to respond appropriately when a vendor, regulatory body, or internal process has identified a product or equipment defect that could pose a risk, including notification, removal, repair, or replacement.

#### Reverse Logistics

The ability to plan and execute the movement of goods from their point of consumption back through the supply chain for return, repair, recycling, or disposal.

#### Store Replenishment

The ability to determine optimal quantities and timing for restocking products at service delivery locations based on demand patterns, capacity constraints, and service level targets.

#### Supplier Management

The ability to strategically plan for, and manage, all of the interactions with an organization that supplies goods and/or services to the organizartion, in order to maximize the value of those interactions.  (Also known as Supplier Relationship Management.)

### Merchandising

#### Category Management

The ability to define, organize, and optimize groupings of related products or services to maximize value for customers and the organization.

#### Pricing Managment

The ability to establish, maintain, and adjust the monetary value assigned to products or services based on costs, market conditions, competitive positioning, and strategic objectives.

#### Promotion Management

The ability to plan, execute, and measure time-limited offers, incentives, and campaigns designed to stimulate demand or influence stakeholder behaviour.

#### Store Layout Management

The ability to design and optimize the physical or digital arrangement of products, services, and navigation elements to enhance the experience and achieve business objectives.

## Planning and Strategy

### Performance

#### Forecasting

The ability to use historic data to predict future states or trends, particularly in demand for services and resources, and to support efforts such as budgeting.

#### Investment Portfolio Management

The ability to manage a portfolio (e.g., application, service, resource, project) to achieve a specific investment objective, taking into account investment mix and policy, corporate strategies, risk, and performance objectives.

#### Performance Management

The ability to assess performance of an organization, department, employee, or service, against targets to improve performance and to assess improvement.

#### Initiative Management

The ability to initiate, plan, execute, control, and complete a time-limited work effort of a team to achieve specific goals and meet specific success criteria.

#### Policy Management

The ability to develop, implement, maintain, and enforce organizational policies and procedures that govern healthcare operations, clinical practices, regulatory compliance, and administrative functions, ensuring consistent adherence to standards and continuous improvement through policy lifecycle management.

#### Relationship Management

The ability to manage and analyze interactions with customers and other stakeholders throughout the relationship lifecycle, with the goal of improving satisfaction, loyalty, and mutual value.

### Strategic Planning

#### Capacity Allocation

The ability to assure that the resources of the organization, including focus, energy, people, and funding, are distributed in such a way as to drive progress toward achievement of strategic aims.

#### Enterprise Architecture

The ability to design, govern, and evolve the comprehensive blueprint of organizational structure, business processes, information systems, and technology infrastructure to ensure alignment between business strategy and IT capabilities while supporting healthcare interoperability, scalability, and regulatory compliance.

#### Real Estate Strategy

The ability to plan, acquire, develop, and optimize the portfolio of physical locations and facilities required to support organizational operations and growth.

#### Scenario Analysis

The ability to analyze possible future events by considering alternative possible outcomes.

#### Workforce Planning

The ability to optimize the productivity of all employees.

### Marketing

#### Brand Management

The ability to define, develop, protect, and evolve the identity, reputation, and perceived value of the organization, its products, or its services in the minds of stakeholders.

#### Campaign Management

The ability to plan, execute, and measure targeted communication and outreach initiatives to promote health awareness, disease prevention, patient engagement, and community health programs, while ensuring compliance with healthcare marketing regulations and patient privacy requirements.

#### Market Research

The ability to gather, analyze, and interpret information about markets, customers, competitors, and external factors to inform strategic and operational decisions.
