## Pizzeria Front of House

Business Capabilities that directly shape the customer experience — from how customers discover and order, to how they receive and pay for their food. These business capabilities determine what customers see, feel, and remember about every interaction with the pizzeria.
### Pizzeria Customer Experience

Capabilities focused on the ongoing relationship with the customer — building profiles, deepening engagement, capturing preferences, and rewarding loyalty. These capabilities turn one-time visitors into regulars.
#### Account Management

The ability to create, maintain, and manage customer account records, including contact information, order history, payment preferences, and loyalty programme status, to enable personalised service and support repeat business engagement.
#### Customer Engagement

The ability to initiate, maintain, and deepen interactions with customers through targeted communications, events, and experiences that build loyalty and advocacy.
#### Preference Management

The ability to capture, store, and apply individual stakeholder choices regarding communication methods, product attributes, service options, and privacy settings.
#### Feedback Management

The ability to solicit, capture, analyze, and act upon opinions, ratings, and suggestions from customers, employees, or other stakeholders to drive continuous improvement.
#### Loyalty Program Management

The ability to design, administer, and optimize programs that reward customers for repeat engagement, purchases, or behaviours that benefit the organization.
### Pizzaria Service Delivery

Capabilities that govern how customer requests are received, sequenced, and fulfilled across dine-in, takeaway, and delivery channels. This is where a customer's order becomes a commitment the pizzeria must keep.

#### Appointment Management

The ability to schedule, reschedule, and cancel time-based commitments between the organization and customers, employees, or other parties, optimizing resource utilization and stakeholder convenience.
#### Order Fulfillment

The ability to pick, pack, and prepare orders for delivery or customer collection, coordinating resources to meet service commitments.
#### Order Management

The ability for authorized person to direct or coordinate the provision of services through a series of authorized service requests.
#### Transaction Processing

The ability to capture, validate, authorize, and complete the exchange of value between parties, including payment acceptance and receipt generation.
#### Waitlist Management

The ability to manage and communicate customer queue positions for table seating, pick-up windows, or delivery slots, ensuring fair and transparent sequencing based on arrival order, reservation status, and available capacity.
### Pizzaria Merchandising

Capabilities that shape how the pizzeria presents and prices its products. From organising the menu into coherent categories to managing promotions and pricing strategy, these capabilities directly influence what customers choose and how much they spend.

#### Category Management

The ability to define, organize, and optimize groupings of related products or services to maximize value for customers and the organization.
#### Product Information Management

The ability to create, maintain, enrich, and distribute accurate and consistent information about products or services across all channels and touchpoints.
#### Promotion Management

The ability to plan, execute, and measure time-limited offers, incentives, and campaigns designed to stimulate demand or influence stakeholder behaviour.
#### Pricing Managment

The ability to establish, maintain, and adjust the monetary value assigned to products or services based on costs, market conditions, competitive positioning, and strategic objectives.
## Pizzaria Back of House

Capabilities that keep the operation running behind the scenes. These are the enabling functions that allow front-of-house capabilities to deliver on their promises — covering the physical environment, ingredients, finances, people, and technology that underpin everything.

### Assets

The ability to deploy, operate, maintain, upgrade, and dispose of property owned by the organization and considered to have significant value, in a cost-effective way.
#### Equipment Management

The ability to select, acquire, and use all of the items necessary to deliver services.
#### Environment Management

The ability to maintain the physical conditions and support services of facilities, including cleaning, climate control, and amenities, to enable safe and effective service delivery.
#### Facilities Management

The ability to plan for, acquire, and assure the operation, maintenance, and security of facilities at which services are delivered or other work is performed by employees.
### Knowledge

Capabilities that capture, organise, and apply information generated by the business — surfacing insights from operational data and maintaining the document and records trail that supports accountability and compliance.

#### Analytics

The ability to collect, process, and interpret operational, sales, and customer data through statistical and qualitative analysis, modelling, and reporting to support business decisions including demand forecasting, menu optimisation, and performance improvement.
#### Document Management

The ability to store, manage, and track electronic documents and images of paper-based information.
#### Records Management

The ability to systematically control the creation, storage, retention, and disposition of business records — including orders, transactions, supplier certifications, and compliance documentation — in accordance with regulatory requirements and organizational policies.
### Finance

Capabilities that manage the flow of money into and out of the business — collecting revenue, managing cash, paying suppliers, meeting tax obligations, and tracking what is owed and owing.

#### Accounts Payable

The ability to manage obligations owed to suppliers and employees by tracking, validating, and remitting payments following the receipt of goods or services.
#### Accounts Receivable

The ability for the organization to issue a legally-enforceable claim for payment from a customer or client, including catering orders and corporate accounts.
#### Cash Management

The ability to collect, handle, and use cash, to forecast and manage cash flow, and to optimize the use of cash.
#### Revenue Management

The ability to ensure that debts or obligations are remitted, including those resulting from non-delivery of services or goods under contract.
#### Tax Management

The ability to calculate, report, remit, and optimize tax obligations across all applicable jurisdictions while maintaining compliance with tax laws and regulations.
### Retail Security

Capabilities that protect the business and its customers from fraudulent activity and unauthorised access — covering detection of suspicious transactions and management of digital identities across systems and channels.

#### Fraud Detection

The ability to identify, assess, and prevent fraudulent activities by monitoring, analyzing, and responding to suspicious transactions or behaviours across systems, channels, or processes.
#### Identity Management

The ability to ascertain a distinct, unique identity for a person, as expressed by an identifier that is unique within a certain scope, given characteristics about that individual such as his or her name, date of birth, and gender.
### Retail Supply Chain

Capabilities governing the relationships and processes that keep ingredients, materials, and equipment flowing reliably into the business — from identifying and contracting with suppliers to managing inventory levels and day-to-day procurement.

#### Contract Management

The ability to negotiate, monitor, renew, and end contracts with vendors and suppliers, acting on behalf of the organization when addressing contract terms, product cost, quality, and safety.
#### Inventory Management

The ability to oversee and control the receipt, storage, movement, and use of goods and materials required for the delivery of products or services.
#### Procurement

The ability to identify needs, source suppliers, negotiate terms, and acquire goods or services required for organizational operations.
#### Supplier Management

The ability to strategically plan for, and manage, all of the interactions with an organization that supplies goods and/or services to the organizartion, in order to maximize the value of those interactions.  (Also known as Supplier Relationship Management.)
### Human Resources

Capabilities that manage the people who make the pizzeria run — finding and hiring talent, scheduling shifts, and tracking time worked. In a service business, workforce quality and coverage directly determines service quality.

#### Recruitment

The ability to identify, attract, evaluate, and select qualified candidates for positions within the organization, while ensuring compliance with applicable regulations and organizational diversity and inclusion objectives.
#### Staff Scheduling

The ability to create, manage, and optimise work shift assignments for kitchen, service, and delivery staff across all roles, ensuring adequate coverage during peak service periods while balancing staff availability, skill requirements, and applicable employment standards.
#### Time Keeping

The ability to accurately track, record, and manage employee work hours, attendance, and time-off requests, ensuring compliance with labour regulations and supporting payroll processing and workforce management analytics.
### Operations

Capabilities specific to the production of food — governing recipes, ensuring quality, and managing allergen information that protects customers. These are the capabilities most distinctive to the restaurant industry and most central to the product being delivered.

#### Recipe Management

The ability to create, version, and govern the standardised formulas, preparation methods, and presentation specifications for all menu items, ensuring consistency in product quality, supporting staff training, enabling accurate cost calculation, and maintaining documented allergen information.

#### Quality Assurance

The ability to achieve and maintain a desired level of performance through planning, monitoring, control, and continual improvement of products, services, and processes.
#### Allergy Management

The ability to identify, document, and communicate allergen information across all menu items, ensuring that food preparation, service, and customer communications appropriately address dietary restrictions and comply with applicable food safety regulations.
### Information Technology

Capabilities that plan, deploy, and manage the digital infrastructure supporting the business — from the software applications staff use daily to the integrations between systems and the broader technology estate.

#### Application Management

The ability to oversee the complete lifecycle of software applications across the organization, including portfolio planning, deployment, maintenance, integration, security, and retirement, ensuring applications effectively support operational and customer-facing workflows.
#### Information Exchange

The ability to transmit, receive, and synchronize information between systems, applications, organizations, and external entities through various integration patterns including application programming interfaces, batch file transfers, message queues, and manual workflows.
#### Technology Management

The ability to strategically plan, implement, and govern enterprise technology infrastructure and emerging technologies, including technology assessment, architecture planning, vendor management, and technology roadmapping, ensuring investments align with organizational objectives and operational requirements.
## Pizzaria Planning and Strategy

Capabilities that determine where the business is going and how it will get there. These functions sit above day-to-day operations, setting direction, measuring performance, allocating resources, and building market presence.

### Performance

Capabilities focused on measuring and anticipating how well the business is doing — turning historical data into forecasts and assessing results against targets to close the loop between strategy and execution.

#### Forecasting

The ability to use historic data to predict future states or trends, particularly in demand for services and resources, and to support efforts such as budgeting.
#### Performance Management

The ability to assess performance of an organization, department, employee, or service, against targets to improve performance and to assess improvement.
### Strategic Planning

Capabilities that translate strategic direction into operational choices — deciding where to focus resources, how to plan the workforce, and how to allocate capacity across competing priorities.

#### Capacity Allocation

The ability to assure that the resources of the organization, including focus, energy, people, and funding, are distributed in such a way as to drive progress toward achievement of strategic aims.
#### Workforce Planning

The ability to optimize the productivity of all employees.
### Marketing

Capabilities that build and protect the pizzeria's presence in the market — from managing brand identity and conducting market research to planning and executing campaigns that drive awareness and customer traffic.

#### Brand Management

The ability to define, develop, protect, and evolve the identity, reputation, and perceived value of the organization, its products, or its services in the minds of stakeholders.
#### Campaign Management

The ability to plan, execute, and measure targeted marketing and promotional initiatives across digital and physical channels to drive customer acquisition, increase order frequency, support seasonal promotions, and build brand presence within target markets.
#### Market Research

The ability to gather, analyze, and interpret information about markets, customers, competitors, and external factors to inform strategic and operational decisions.
