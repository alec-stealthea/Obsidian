## Emergency Department Quadruple Aim Measures
The following are potential measures illustrated in this 
![[Emergency Department Quadruple Aim Measures.png]]
- **Enhancing Patient Safety** - It may seem obvious, but many of the patient experience measures have a direct correlation to the end of each of the value stages within the value stream.
	- **Triage wait time** - This is measured from the time of 'arrival' as part of registration until the triage score has been assigned by a care provider. This also signals the end of the Triage Value Stage.
	- **Time to Diagnosis** - Measured by the entry of a diagnosis that may support or change the presenting concern based on the time from initial registration. This is usually the wait time that is advertised to patients in the waiting room or looking at wait times that may be provided to the public.
	- **Time to Treatment** - When treatment starts as indicated by either the confirmation of the care plan or the entry of the provider into the treatment room. 
	- **Total Encounter Time** - Total encounter time from the patient's perspective will be the time they are released to care for themselves at home or transferred to another care setting within the hospital.
- **Improving the Work-life Experience of Providers** - Many times the provider measures are based on subjective assessments that may or may not be supported by applications.
	- **Workload** - ensuring appropriate care team capacity available based on ED load
	- **Standard of Care** - 
	- **Quality of Care** - Measure of reported patient safety incidents by staff or patients.
	- **Staff Safety** - Measure of reported provider safety incidents
- **Improving Population Health**
	- **infectious diseases Isolation** - Monitoring the time from registration to isolation when a person is sustected of or is confirmed to have an infectious disease. 
	- **Mortality Rate** - Percentage of encounters that resulted in a fatality.
	- **Left without being seen** - Number of patients who register, but leave before the encounter is finished.
	- **Non-Emergent Patients** - Percentage of patients that could have been treated in another care setting. 
- **Reducing Healthcare Costs**
	- **Readmission rate** - Number of patients who return to the emergency department within 24 hours of last being registered.
	- **Correct diagnosis** - Percentage of patients whose diagnosis was changed in later care encounters. 
	- **Appropriate treatment** - A review to ensure that the most cost-effective appropriate treatments are being administered usually based on peer review.
	- **Probability of Admission** - Determining at triage which patients are of a high probability of being admitted to further hospital treatments.

Many of these measures require additional measures to be able to evaluate the emergency department value stream at scale. For example, the overall ED occupancy within the emergency department combined with the acuity assessment, provider capacity, presenting complaint, and space utilization are required to be able to predict wait times.
