With the core [[Emergency Department Walk-in Value Stream Analysis]] completed, we are then able to review variations that may adjust one or more of the value stages or possibly the entire value stream.  More often than not, variance will be at the value stage level vs. the entire value stream. Value stream capability instances may also be introduced.  A key aspect for deciding in how many value stream scenarios that need to be examined will be the overall volume of that particular variation.

For our analysis we will look at three. First and most obvious will be the Emergency Medical Services scenario.  A second scenario will be a pediatric patient followed, finally by a rural scenario.
## Emergency Medical Services Transfer
Emergency Medical Services transfers increasingly will be an anticipated event that can be called in over radio, or increasingly, with a pre-admit request coming in electronically.  The electronic pre-admit may already include much of the information to have the patient moved immediately to the third emergency department value stream stage where they would be reassessed.

For some hospitals, there is a special waiting area for EMS transfers which may leave then in the triage state for longer until there is capacity to move them to the next stage.

Another aspect of EMS transfers will be the situation where there is a multiple casualty event. In this situation, advance notice may trigger changes to the patients who are currently in the emergency to be re-prioritized to make space. It may also cause on-call resources to be notified to staff the emergency room to increase the ability for the emergancy department to manage more concurrent value streams.
## Pediatric Emergency Department Value Stream
The pediatric value stream will remain (largely) unchanged from the overall value stream - though the capability instances - especially with respect to the clinical assessment scores will be changed to reflect the difference in treating children.

For larger hospitals or dedicated pediatric hospitals, there may be dedicated treatment value stream stages. Broken bones are more common within the pediatric cohort, so being able to triage and move patients to a specific casting treatment value stream stage allows for specialization and optimization with dedicated staff and resources for that particular therapy.
