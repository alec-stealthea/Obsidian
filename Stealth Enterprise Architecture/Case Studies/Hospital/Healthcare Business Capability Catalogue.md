## Healthcare Business Capability Catalogue

Healthcare Business Capability Catalogue

## Clinical Care

### Care Delivery

#### Allergy Management

The ability to identify, track, and ensure that care plans and activities take appropriate account of a patient's immune system hypersensitivities to environmental factors.

#### Case Management

The coordination of services, in accordance with a care plan, on behalf of a patient or family across various care settings and providers.  Coordination activities usually involve the health care system, but may also touch on services such as rehabilitation, social work, housing, employment, and justice.   The aim of case management is to assure that appropriate care and supportive services are provided to patients.
#### Chronic Disease Management

The ability to coordinate services so that people with chronic diseases, especially those that are long-lasting, persistent, and which require special therapy, education, and training, can achieve optimal health outcomes​.

#### Clinical Decision Management

The ability to deliver pertinent, organized clinical knowledge and patient information to healthcare providers at the point of care in order to enhance their patient care decisions and actions.

#### Consent Management

The ability to manage and document patient consent for various medical treatments, procedures, and data sharing activities, ensuring that consent is obtained, recorded, and updated in accordance with legal, regulatory, and ethical standards. This capability includes tracking consent status, managing consent preferences, and ensuring that healthcare providers are aware of and comply with patients’ consent choices.

#### Encounter Management

#### Medication Management

The ability to deliver safe, effective and appropriate drug therapy, to maximize patient benefit and outcomes.

#### Personal Health Management

The ability to effectively engage people in the management of their health care, by providing access to their electronic health record.

#### Result Management

### Care Planning

#### Acuity Management

The ability to assess the severity or complexity of illness, in order to allocate appropriate medical (usually nursing) resources for an individual patient, or a group of patients.

#### Care Team Coordination

The ability to select, assemble, and coordinate the efforts of various health care and other service providers to execute on the patient's care plan.

#### Clinical Assessment

The ability of a care provider to identify the specific needs and problems, and health status, including disease or condition progression and bio-psychosocial status, of a person seeking health care, usually by interview, self-report of the presenting problem, physical assessment, clinical and family history, and various assessment tools and tests (lab, imaging).

#### Clinical Diagnosis

The ability to identify the nature or cause of a disease or condition based on clinical assessment, and diagnostic test results, and to assign it to a generally-accepted set of diagnostic categories​.

#### Consultation

The ability of two or more health care professionals to deliberate about and evaluate a patient’s diagnosis, prognosis, and/or indicated treatment.​

#### Eligibility Management

#### Inter-Agency Coordination

The ability to coordinate services provided by different agencies or ministries (housing, for example) to address health-related needs and the social recovery of the patient.

#### Order Management

The ability for authorized care providers to direct or coordinate the provision of services through a series of authorized service requests.

#### Patient Consultation

#### Referral Management

The ability of a provider to request on behalf of his patient, the services of another provider without interruption in service or loss of assessment or other information about the patient.

#### Transition Management

The ability to ensure the safe and effective coordination and continuity of care as clients experience a change in health status, care needs, service provider, or care setting.

#### Utilization Management

The ability to manage health care costs by influencing patient care decision-making through case-by-case assessments of the appropriateness of care prior to its provision.  [adapted from Institute of Medicine Committee definition, 1989]

### Standards of Care

#### Accreditation Management

The ability of health care organizations or departments to manage the processes involved in obtaining certification of competency, authority, or credibility by an accreditation body.

#### Care Pathways

The ability to define and deliver the care expected for patients with a specific condition or symptoms, in an appropriate time frame.

#### Clinical Documentation

The ability to capture, produce, and use a complete and accurate record of clinically relevant information gathered by care providers in the course of planning, delivering, and monitoring the progress of care.  Possible uses include clinical decision support, analytics, future care planning by this or other care providers, and research.

#### Formulary Management

## Clinical and Corporate Operations

### Assets

The ability to deploy, operate, maintain, upgrade, and dispose of property owned by the organization and considered to have significant value, in a cost-effective way.

#### Equipment Management

The ability to select, acquire, and use all of the items necessary to deliver services.

#### Environment Management

#### Facilities Management

The ability to plan for, acquire, assure the operation of , maintenance of, and security of facilities at which care is delivered or other work performed by employees.

#### Fleet Management

### Human Resources

#### Academic Appointment Management

The ability to manage the clinical and administrative workload and activities of care providers that also have responsibilities related to their academic appointments as professors, researchers, and administrative personnel.

#### Education Management

The ability to develop, deliver, track, and certify clinical training programs and educational content for healthcare professionals, including mandatory certifications, continuing education requirements, competency assessments, and specialized clinical skills development to ensure safe and effective patient care.

#### Employee Relations

#### Compensation Management

The ability to provide a person with money or other economic value in exchange for goods, labor, or to provide for costs incurred due to injury or travel, for example.

#### Learning Management

#### Recruitment

#### Staff Scheduling

The ability to create, manage, and optimize work shift assignments for healthcare personnel across departments and care units, ensuring adequate coverage while balancing staff preferences, competency requirements, regulatory mandates, and operational efficiency.

#### Talent Management

Activities and efforts by an employee and his or her employer to increase existing job-relevant skills and knowledge.

#### Time Keeping

The ability to accurately track, record, and manage employee work hours, attendance, and time-off for healthcare staff, ensuring compliance with labor regulations, union agreements, and healthcare staffing requirements while supporting payroll processing and workforce analytics.

### Capacity

#### Bed Management

The ability to predict and coordinate the availability of care settings and assignment of patients across the care continuum, ensuring that the right patients are in the most cost effective facilities that will provide the right care, for the right time.

#### Delivery Site Management

The ability manage sites at which care is delivered.

#### Dispatch Management

#### Patient Location Management

#### Patient Transport

#### Service Management

#### Waitlist Management

The ability to manage the order in which health care services can be scheduled for individual patients, ordinarily based on urgency, availability of resources, and order of request.

### Medical Devices

#### Medical Device Management

The ability to select, acquire, certify. deploy, maintain, and use electronic and mechanical equipment designed to support diagnosis, monitoring, and care delivery, including implants.

#### Instrument Management

The ability to select, acquire, and use the tools required for measurement, e.g., laboratory and imaging systems.

### Patient

#### Home Monitoring

Ability of a patient to use one or more mobile medical devices or self-assessment tools to perform routine tests and send the results to a healthcare professional.

#### Patient Transport 2

The ability to transport patients to hospitals and other care settings, and between different care settings.

### Information Technology

#### Application Management

The ability to oversee the complete lifecycle of software applications within the healthcare organization, including application portfolio planning, deployment, maintenance, integration, security, performance optimization, and retirement, ensuring applications support clinical and operational workflows while maintaining compliance with healthcare regulations.

#### Information Exchange

the ability to facilitate, monitor, and control the exchange of data and information across internal and external systems, stakeholders, and processes. It encompasses the coordination of data standards, protocols, and security measures to ensure timely, accurate, and secure information flow.

#### Technology Management

The ability to strategically plan, implement, and govern enterprise technology infrastructure and emerging technologies to support healthcare delivery, including technology assessment, architecture planning, vendor management, technology roadmapping, and ensuring technology investments align with organizational objectives and regulatory requirements.

### Procurement

#### Contract Management

The abilty to negotiate, monitor, renew, and end contracts with vendors and suppliers, including contracted health care providers, acting on behalf of the organization and patient interests when addressing contract terms, product cost, quality, and safety.

#### Inventory Management

#### Supplier Management

The ability to strategically plan for, and manage, all of the interactions with an organization that supplies goods and/or services to the organizartion, in order to maximize the value of those interactions.  (Also known as Supplier Relationship Management.)

#### Operator Management

The ability to strategically plan for, and manage, all of the interactions with patner organizations that provide health care facilities and services to the organization under contract and regulatory oversight.

#### Recall Management

#### Service Management 2

The ability to coordinate and oversee the delivery of corporate services to ensure they meet the needs of the organisation and its stakeholders, including managing service quality, availability, and performance to optimise operational efficiency and support business objectives.

### Finance

#### Accounts Payable

The ability to leverage a form of credit extended by suppliers, employees, and patients, by allowing payment to follow the receipt of products or services.

#### Accounts Receivable

The ability for the organization to issue a legally-enforceable claim for payment from a customer, client, or patient.

#### Activity-based Costing

#### Activity-based Funding

#### Cash Management

The ability to collect, handle, and use cash, to forecast and manage cash flow, and to optimize the use of cash.

#### Claims Management

#### Financial Accounting

#### Fundraising Management

#### Grant Management

The ability to discover grant sources; to submit grant proposals; to budget initiatives that are funded [in part] by one or more grants; to assure that grant funds are spent in a way that meets legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements; to produce disbursement requests; to track expenditures against the approved budget; to produce change requests; and to produce required reporting for the grantors.

#### Revenue Management

The ability to ensure that debts or obligations are remitted, including those resulting from non-delivery of services or goods under contract.

#### Tax Management

#### Trust Account Management

### Knowledge

#### Analytics

Ability to use population health and operational data, statistical and qualitative analysis, and explanatory and predictive modeling to drive health care policy and health care system decision making.

#### Content Managemnent

#### Document Management

The ability to store, manage, and track electronic documents and images of paper-based information.

#### Health Information Management

#### Records Management

The capability to systematically control the creation, distribution, use, maintenance, and disposition of the legal health record, i.e., recorded information maintained as evidence of health care activities and transactions.

#### Master Data Management

The ability to create, manage, and distribute the information pertaining to business entities (e.g., providers, patients, care delivery sites, and services) that are agreed upon and shared across the health system; as well as reference data (value sets, vocabularies, terminologies) that are shared -- nationally or internationally -- among health systems.

#### Translation

### Research

#### Clinical Trial Management

The ability to manage the planning, approval (including ethics board approval, for example), patient recruitment, consent, study implementation, reporting, and completion for a clinical trial, a research study intended to explore a new medical device, strategy, or treatment.

### Risk

#### Business Continuity Management

The ability to identify risks, threats, and vulnerabilities that could affect an organization's continued operations and provide a framework for building organizational resilience and the capability for an effective response.

#### Emergency / Disaster Management

The ability to create and execute plans to reduce an organization's vulnerability to hazards and to improve the ability to cope with disasters.

#### Fraud Detection

#### Identity Management

The ability to ascertain a distinct, unique identity for a person, as expressed by an identifier that is unique within a certain scope, given characteristics about that individual such as his or her name, date of birth, and gender.

#### Incident Management

The ability to identify, respond to, investigate, and resolve adverse events, safety incidents, security breaches, and operational disruptions within the healthcare environment, including root cause analysis, corrective actions, and preventive measures to ensure patient safety and organizational resilience.

#### Information Privacy Management

The ability to determine what information can be shared with a third party.

#### Legal & Regulatory Compliance

The ability to assure and demonstrate conformance to all laws and regulations applicable to the health system.

## Planning and Strategy

### Performance

#### Forecasting

The ability to use historic data to predict future states or trends, particularly in demand for services and resources, and to support efforts such as budgeting.

#### Investment Portfolio Management

The ability to manage a portfolio (e.g., application, service, resource, project) to achieve a specific investment objective, taking into account investment mix and policy, corporate strategies, risk, and performance objectives.

#### Performance Management

The ability to assess performance of an organization, department, employee, or service, against targets to improve performance and to assess improvement.

#### Initiative Management

The ability to initiate, plan, execute, control, and complete a time-limited work effort of a team to achieve specific goals and meet specific success criteria.

#### Policy Management

The ability to develop, implement, maintain, and enforce organizational policies and procedures that govern healthcare operations, clinical practices, regulatory compliance, and administrative functions, ensuring consistent adherence to standards and continuous improvement through policy lifecycle management.

#### Relationship Management

### Population and Public Health

#### Campaign Management

The ability to plan, execute, and measure targeted communication and outreach initiatives to promote health awareness, disease prevention, patient engagement, and community health programs, while ensuring compliance with healthcare marketing regulations and patient privacy requirements.

#### Citizen Engagement

The ability of the health care system to Interact and consult with citizens to ensure they have an informed say in the health system decisions that affect their lives.

#### Community Surveillance

The ability to monitor communities for evidence of emerging threats to residents' health, to gather information on behalf of the health system, and to prepare to respond

#### Disease Prevention

The ability to avoid illness, injury, and other health-related problems by changing the environment, providing information, and directing action at the underlying causes of the problems.

#### Epidemiology

The ability to understand and affect the incidence, distribution, and possible control of diseases and other factors relating to health.​

#### Environmental Health Managememt

#### Immunization Mgmt

The ability to control and eliminate life-threatening infectious diseases in vulnerable populations through the use of immunization, the process whereby a person is made immune or resistant to an infectious disease.

#### Outbreak Management

The ability to detect a sudden increase in occurrences of a disease in a particular time and place, and to respond in a way that identifies the cause of the outbreak, limits the further spread of the disease, and protects the health of the population.

#### Population Health

#### Surveillance

Ability to monitor behavior and specific health conditions and diseases in order to influence and manage health care systems.

### Strategic Planning

#### Capacity Allocation

The ability to assure that the resources of the organization, including focus, energy, people, and funding, are distributed in such a way as to drive progress toward achievement of strategic aims.

#### Enterprise Architecture

The ability to design, govern, and evolve the comprehensive blueprint of organizational structure, business processes, information systems, and technology infrastructure to ensure alignment between business strategy and IT capabilities while supporting healthcare interoperability, scalability, and regulatory compliance.

#### Health Business Planning

The ability to devise strategies and programs for addressing public health risks, based on the analysis of health, demographic, and other information.

#### Scenario Analysis

The ability to analyze possible future events by considering alternative possible outcomes.

#### Workforce Planning

The ability to optimize the productivity of all employees.
