A **domain graph** (often called a _domain knowledge graph_ or _ontology_) models knowledge and concepts specific to a subject area or domain. For example, in a domain graph about healthcare:
• **Nodes** represent concepts or entities (e.g., _patient_, _disease_, _diagnosis_, _treatment plan_).
• **Edges** express relationships or properties—such as “has symptom,” “is treated by,” “is associated with.”

**Key Features**

• **Domain-focused**: Instead of focusing on word-level semantic relationships, it captures facts, categories, and rules unique to a given area (e.g., biology, finance, manufacturing).
• **Entity-centric**: Tends to focus on specific instances or classes of things, such as products, people, or organizational units.
• **Supports reasoning**: Advanced domain graphs often incorporate rules (e.g., constraints, inheritance) allowing for automated reasoning or inference.
