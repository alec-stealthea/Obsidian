A node derives from the term knot, as might be applicable in a net. Mathematicians liken nodes to points, and as such, it's useful to think of them as points of interest in what is typically called graph theory. From a graph, standpoint, nodes are usually considered entities, states or typed values. In RDF, nodes are typically associated with entities or concepts and are called subjects or objects.
