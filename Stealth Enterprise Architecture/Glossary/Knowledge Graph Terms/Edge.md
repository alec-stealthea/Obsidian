An edge is the line that connects two or more nodes. Typically edges may also have some kind of identifier associated with them. A node can exist without edges, but an edge always has at least two nodes. In RDF, edges are typically associated with a single relationship or attribute and is called predicates. In a property graph, edges usually have a similar association.
