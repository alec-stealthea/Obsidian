If we’re truly wanting to be an agile enterprise, continuous improvement of the core value streams we execute needs to be a foundational part of how we operate.  Within most agile frameworks, the Epic is intended to represent a large body of work to enhance a product.  That usually leads to a form of [[Enterprise Agile Product Roadmap]]. 

What if we change that paradigm? 

What if - for business domains, the agile Epic represents the [[Value Streams]] for that particular domain? Rather than a short-term means to accomplish work for an application, it becomes the means to ground in the business outcomes we’re hoping to achieve within the products we use to enable those value streams. Now, Epics form the basis for [[Domain Investment Portfolio Management]] with the business domains.

Taking it to the next level down, a domain’s enterprise agile Feature is one of the Value Stages. Finally, the Enterprise Agile Stories become the business capability improvements the domain is hoping for.  At this more atomic level, a business capability being a combination of people, processes and tools becomes the basis for writing the [[Agile Story]] as follows:

As a (role) I need (the following capability process and/or tool enhancement) so that (I can achieve a desired outcome).

Sometimes those stories will be able to be managed by the Domain’s [[Enterprise Agile Team]].  Other times they will need to rely on Product Teams or if this impacts the larger [[Value Stream Network]], another Domain’s Value Stream.

So how does that become a Domain Road map?  Ideas are encouraged to be as small as possible to support quick iteration of continuous improvement.  This backlog of ideas gets prioritized based on the value derived across all the value streams impacted, and then scheduled into monthly iterations and the eventual ‘release’ of the business capability enhancements.
