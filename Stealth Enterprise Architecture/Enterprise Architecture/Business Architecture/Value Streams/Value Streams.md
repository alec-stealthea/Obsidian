A Value Stream provides one of the most valuable models of a business architecture. It describes the way an organization delivers something of value to one of their stakeholders. This can be products or services. As a model it's quite simple, but with some very powerful rules that make it compelling to support your business architecture practice.

Note - the next few posts are going to be going down a business architecture rabbit hole vs. the lighter side of architecture from my earlier posts. Further note, I am using as an example an industry that I have never been a business architect. I think healthcare business architecture is a bit esoteric, so for the financial sector enterprise architects - please read the post [[Stealth EA/Blog Entries/An Argue of Architects?]] before commenting.
#### Who's Receiving the Value?

When I review much of business architecture, it seems that the focus is on the stakeholder group who's looking to deliver the product or service rather than the person who wants the product or service. Putting the customer first may seem straight forward or even something taken for granted. Look more closely at many of the models that we see about a business, and they are usually from the perspective of the people who generated the model.

So rule # 1 of the value stream model is that it's from the perspective of the person receiving the value. So what does that mean? First off, make sure that you know who the stakeholder receiving the value is. This may again seem evident, but it's sometimes not as obvious as people think.

For this example, let's take an example from the finance industry and look at getting a home mortgage. This may be quite complicated with couples in a joint application or situations where there's people guaranteeing the mortgage, but for simplicity let's call the stakeholder receiving the value the homeowner.
#### Triggering the Value Stream
How does the person receiving the value start the value stream in motion? This may seem obvious, but there are many times when there are proxies for the people receiving the value who start value streams. Usually, there's more than one trigger but is can be as simple as one. In out mortgage situation, the homeowner could phone, go online, or even show up in person.
#### Stages of Value
Here comes the really crucial part - delineating value stages. I find that this is where the real fun begins because most of the time the stages articulated by the business are the internal processes or stages.  My question when they create the first draft of the stages is always - and what's the value to the receiving stakeholder?  Invariably, this results in much discussion and typically a shift in understanding and perspective.

I use a presentation application modelling tools or sometimes a more dedicated drawing application a lot of the time at the early stages of consultation. For these examples I will be using the [ArchiMate](https://pubs.opengroup.org/architecture/archimate3-doc/) language and models generated using the open-source tool [Archi](https://www.archimatetool.com/).
