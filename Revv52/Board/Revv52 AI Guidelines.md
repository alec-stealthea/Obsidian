
## Purpose

Revv52 is built on human artistry — 52 voices on mics, a live band, and the creative energy of real performers delivering high-energy shows. As AI tools become more accessible, we want to be intentional about how we use them: embracing efficiency where it helps, while protecting the human creativity that defines who we are.

These guidelines establish clear boundaries for AI use across the organization, ensuring that technology serves as a tool to support — never replace — the artists, designers, musicians, and volunteers who make Revv52 possible.

---

## Guiding Principles

1. **Human artistry comes first.** Every Revv52 performance is a live, human experience. AI cannot replace the expression, emotion, and connection that our performers bring to the stage.

2. **AI is a tool, not a creator.** AI may assist with workflows and prototyping, but final creative output must reflect substantial human authorship, judgment, and oversight.

3. **Protect artists and their livelihoods.** AI use must not displace paid opportunities for graphic designers, vocalists, instrumentalists, arrangers, or other creative professionals who support our productions.

4. **Be transparent.** When AI is used in any externally shared material, we disclose it. Our audiences and collaborators deserve to know.

5. **Respect intellectual property.** We do not use AI tools to replicate the style, voice, likeness, or creative expression of any identifiable artist without their written consent.

---

## Permitted Uses

The following AI uses are approved and do not require additional authorization:

- **Rehearsal tracks:** AI-generated part-specific rehearsal recordings (e.g., isolated tenor, alto, soprano, bass tracks) to help members learn their parts between rehearsals.
- **Prototype graphics:** AI-generated draft concepts for marketing visuals, used as direction-setting references during the design process — not as final deliverables.
- **Marketing copy drafts:** AI-assisted drafting of social media posts, email campaigns, program notes, and promotional copy, subject to human review and editing before publication.
- **Administrative tasks:** Meeting note summaries, scheduling assistance, data analysis, budget modelling, and other operational support.
- **Research and brainstorming:** Using AI to explore creative concepts, generate setlist ideas, research repertoire, or draft strategic documents.

---

## Restricted Uses (Require Board or Committee Approval)

The following uses require written approval from the relevant committee chair or the Board before proceeding:

- Using AI-generated visuals in any final, public-facing materials (posters, social media graphics, programs, merchandise) rather than commissioning human designers.
- Using AI-generated audio in any public performance or recording beyond internal rehearsal tracks.
- Inputting any member's or collaborator's likeness, voice recording, or personal data into AI tools.
- Any use of AI that would replace a paid or volunteer creative role.

---

## Prohibited Uses

The following are not permitted under any circumstances:

- **Replacing live performers.** AI-generated vocals, instrumentals, or performances may not be used in any Revv52 show, concert, or public-facing recording in place of human performers.
- **Uncredited final creative output.** Publishing AI-generated graphics, copy, or other creative work as final output without disclosure and without substantial human creative contribution.
- **Imitating artists.** Using AI to replicate the voice, likeness, or distinctive style of any identifiable living artist without their explicit written consent.
- **Unauthorized data use.** Uploading members' personal information, voice recordings, photographs, or creative work into AI training systems or third-party AI platforms without explicit consent.

---

## Disclosure Standards

When AI tools have been used in the creation of any publicly shared material, Revv52 will include a brief disclosure. Examples:

- *"Design concept developed with AI assistance; final artwork by [Artist Name]."*
- *"Rehearsal tracks created using AI-generated audio to support member preparation."*
- *"Draft copy assisted by AI; reviewed and edited by the Revv52 marketing committee."*

The specific language may vary, but the intent is always transparency about AI's role in our process.

---

## Commitment to Creative Professionals

Revv52 values the artists, designers, and musicians in our community. This policy commits us to:

- Continuing to engage human graphic designers for final show artwork, posters, and branding materials.
- Hiring live musicians and working with human arrangers for all performances.
- Compensating creative professionals fairly for their work.
- Treating AI-generated prototypes as starting points for human creatives, not replacements for them.

When budget allows, we will prioritize engaging local Calgary artists and creatives.

---

## Review and Updates

These guidelines will be reviewed annually by the Board of Directors, or sooner if significant changes in AI technology or organizational needs warrant it. Members, volunteers, and collaborators are encouraged to raise questions or concerns about AI use with any Board member.
