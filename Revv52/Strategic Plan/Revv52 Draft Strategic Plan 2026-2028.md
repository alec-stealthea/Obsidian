## 1. Situation Assessment

### Context and Background

In 2008, the Calgary Choral Society rebranded itself as Revv52 to reflect a shift from traditional choral performances to popular music. The name combines "Revv" (energy and revolution) with "52" (founding year and number of performers). The inaugural performance, "Revvolution," featured Beatles and Beach Boys covers at the Jubilee Auditorium. Since then, Revv52 has performed biannually, expanding in 2015 to include a fall gala.

### Key Accomplishments 2024

- **Transformed staging:** Moved from traditional risers to dynamic platforms and full-stage use with choreography and storytelling
- **Individual microphones:** Transitioned from 8 shared group mics to 52 individual handheld mics with Midas D boards
- **Member engagement:** Members now central to arranging, rehearsal tracks, choreography, scriptwriting, program writing, video editing, and band. Welcomed 18 new members of diverse ages/backgrounds. Expanded solo opportunities.
- **Digital presence:** Released 36 music videos including Cree/French/English O Canada. YouTube subscribers grew from 700 to 3,000+.
- **Diverse show themes:** Everything New Is Old Again, Times Like These, Game Changers, Our Own Thing, Revvin' Country, World on a String, One Hit Wonders, Epic 80's, On Broadway
- **ReMix sub-ensemble:** ~10 years of community performances supporting local causes. ~$20K net contribution per year; $175K+ total.
- **Collaborations:** Storybook Theatre, illFX Hip Hop, BANC, Yukichi Hattori Dance, CPO, Mount Royal Kantorei, Young Canadians, Brother Bicker Band
- **Vocal development:** Expert coaches, private instruction, workshops, solo opportunities for every member in some concerts
- **Albums:** Sounds of the Season (first Christmas album); backing vocals for Brother Bicker Band; earlier pandemic releases (Virtually Yours Vols 1 & 2)
- **Financial health:** Fully digital financial process. Sustaining Donor Program ($5K/yr x 5 yrs) raised ~$500K YTD; $65K in 2025 fiscal year (16% of revenues). ReMix net contribution $175K+.

### Understanding Calgary's Arts Audiences

Key insights from the Rozsa Foundation "Spotlight on Arts Audiences: Wave 3" (Summer/Fall 2024):

- **Audience Segments:** Diverse engagement levels (immersed, engaged, passive, infrequent) — each requires tailored approaches
- **Experience-Driven Engagement:** Emotional and transformative experiences are key drivers; audiences value personal impact over purely educational aspects
- **Time Constraints:** Calgarians are highly selective; compelling programming and flexible options needed over rigid subscriptions
- **Barriers:** Financial constraints, perceived lack of relevance, downtown accessibility
- **Market Saturation:** Number of arts organizations exceeds what Calgary's population can sustain — differentiation is essential
- **Decision-Making:** Women play dominant role; 48-hour window typical for final ticket purchase — well-timed marketing critical
- **Digital Engagement:** SMS vs email explored; texts recommended for performance reminders

### Notable Performing Arts Initiatives in Calgary

- **Arts Commons Transformation (ACT):** Canada's largest cultural infrastructure initiative. Expected completion ~2028. First phase includes 1,000-seat theatre and 200-seat studio theatre, expanding downtown arts hub by ~45%.
- **Theatre Calgary's "Theatre for All":** Subsidized orchestra-level seats to attract diverse audiences (started 2023-2024 season).

### Immersive and Inclusive Experience Models

The report identifies emerging trends relevant to Revv52's positioning:

- **EDM / Boiler Room sets:** 360-degree layouts, unique venues, intimacy, no-phone raves, daytime raves, sober raving
- **Large-scale festivals (EDC):** Multi-day, multi-sensory, community-oriented (PLUR philosophy)
- **Calgary-specific:** Dos Leches' Sunset Dance Parties — all ages, outdoor, inclusive, outgrowing venues
- **Cross-genre:** CPO's "Synthony" concert (September 2025) blending orchestral music with electronic beats

> These developments illustrate a clear shift: audiences seek events combining high-quality production with intimacy, community, and inclusivity.

### Audience Research Key Findings

*Source: [[Audience Segmentation Proposal - Finley Associates|Audience Engagement Survey]], June 17-July 12, 2025 (n=431)*

- Member and Regular Audience needs are statistically similar
- Audience is primarily female, over 65, white/Caucasian
- 86% live in Calgary; 30% have at least one dependent; 76% in a relationship
- Top entertainment spending: dining out, music concerts, live theatre/travelling shows, physical activities, movies
- 49% connected to Revv52 in some way; 40% follow social media; 35% amateur artists, 10% professional
- 50% love genre-mixing approach; 86% have attended at least one of last five shows
- 20% plan to attend all shows; 32% plan to attend most

**What audiences like most:** High energy and enthusiasm (34%), talent (26%), music selection (21%), innovative arrangements, choreography, solo/group mix

**Areas for improvement:** Sound and technical quality (22%), performance style and choreography (19%), soloist vs ensemble balance (15%), program structure, affordability, venue logistics, clearer marketing

**Most important aspects:** Music selection (94%), staging/lighting quality (85%), value for money (84%), parking, communications, safe inclusive environments

**Highest satisfaction:** Music selection (91%), unique "take" on familiar music (90%), staging/lighting (89%)

**Lowest satisfaction:** Audience engagement (71%), overall "dazzling" experience (69%), social media engagement (39%)

**Over-delivering:** Audience engagement (+28%), unique take on familiar music (+28%)

**Opportunity areas:** Music selection (-3%), parking (-3%)

## 2. Strategic Framework

### Values

Creativity, Connection, Excellence, Collaboration, Community

### Vision

> Canada's leader in innovative vocal arts.

### Mission

> To bring together exceptional, high-caliber performers to create immersive vocal experiences that are as individual as they are collective.

**Key Concepts:**
- Using innovative technology including individual microphones that let audiences hear every voice and the full power of the choir, we reimagine what choral performance can be.
- Our dynamic repertoire, energy, and sense of fun reflect a member-driven ensemble committed to creating and sustaining Canada's most innovative vocal arts community.

### Goals

1. Our brand is widely recognizable
2. Our audience experience is engaging and immersive
3. We understand the evolving landscape
4. Our structure is sustainable
5. We cherish our sense of FUN!
6. We are mindful of our Revv52 community
7. We are innovative and collaborative

### Key Success Factors

1. **Creativity & Innovation** — Original compositions, inventive repertoire, fresh approaches
2. **Talent Development & Member Engagement** — Recruiting, nurturing, retaining high-caliber members
3. **Collaboration & Leadership** — Building partnerships, fostering buy-in, leveraging creative team guidance
4. **Audience & Community Connection** — Growing loyal audiences, engaging wider community, public visibility
5. **Performance Excellence** — Consistently high standards showcasing unique sound, style, and energy
6. **Continuous Improvement & Exposure** — Ongoing refinement to increase reach and impact

### Key Performance Indicators

| KPI Category | Rationale | Measurement Mechanisms | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Financial Sustainability** | Ensure ability to deliver quality productions and sustain operations | Funding diversity ratio; variance to budget; revenue per production; revenue growth; donor retention/acquisition | Quarterly |
| **2. Artistic & Talent Development** | Support continuous growth and performance quality | Member profile (retention, engagement, progression); member satisfaction survey; creative team profile | Annually |
| **3. Community Connection & Audience Engagement** | Measure depth and breadth of public engagement | Audience per performance; audience research (demographics, diversity); community partnerships; audience feedback; social media metrics | Annually |

| **6. Innovation & Differentiation** | Ensure Revv52 stays fresh and distinctive | Comparative analysis vs peer ensembles; audience/member survey on choice drivers; new innovative projects per season | Every 2-3 years |

## 3. Three-Year Implementation Plan

### SP 1 — Audience Development (Growth & Diversification)

> Expand and diversify Revv52's audience by broadening outreach, enhancing accessibility, and creating inclusive, engaging experiences that attract new and returning patrons.

**1.1** Research alternative venues, formats, and options supporting audience growth, diversification, and parking concerns:
- a) Leverage targeted artistic collaborators for joint publicity, audience share, and meaningful ongoing collaboration
- b) Identify and test new venues across diverse Calgary communities (e.g., Evan Hazell Theatre, Rajveer Hall, Genesis Centre, Vivo, RockPointe, regional festivals) using 'promoted' ReMix performances as 'scout team'
- c) Partner with other charities — provide entertainment at their events for funds, profile, and wider audience
- d) Explore House Band Agreement with BMO as initial pilot (flexible musical support from small groups to large ensembles)
- e) Explore food and beverage options in/near performance spaces
- f) Explore expanding performance offerings outside Calgary

**1.2** Reimagine the Gala (faced with declining member participation)

**1.3** Develop additional choir models:
- 'Farm team' choir for skill development
- 'Drop-in' choir for social singing
- 'Pub' choir for informal, alternative performances

**1.4** Update [[Revv52 Marketing Plan 2025-2026 (Updated)|Marketing Plan]] to better reflect recent audience research and emerging channels

**1.5** Encourage open rehearsals to bring in potential stakeholders

### SP 2 — Organizational Sustainability (Member Engagement)

> Strengthen member engagement and ensure organizational sustainability by fostering a supportive, rewarding environment while diversifying revenue and optimizing operations.

**2.1** Conduct annual anonymous Member Engagement Survey administered by third party (longitudinal analysis)

**2.2** Review and update governance structure:
- a) Define clear sequence and priority for hiring paid staff to support operations and enable Board to become more strategic
- b) Consider balancing internal and external Board members

**2.3** Professional development and succession planning:
- a) Succession Plan for key artist roles (Artistic Director, Producer, Production Manager, Technical Director, Audio Engineer, Choreographer, Accompanist)
- b) Succession Plan for Board (term limits, recruitment/nomination, professional development)
- c) Mentorship support for new members

### SP 3 — Artistic Excellence & Innovation

> Advance artistic excellence and innovation by elevating performance quality, embracing creative risk-taking, and developing fresh, distinctive programming.

**3.1** Develop a 3-year Artistic Vision informed by the Strategic Plan

**3.2** Develop centralized Music Library:
- a) Engage composer/songwriter for an original song
- b) Develop high-quality video content to showcase performances and artistic innovation

**3.3** Explore smaller performance groups — outline selection, rehearsal, and featuring processes

**3.4** Leverage technology:
- a) Investigate artistic partnerships with tech companies
- b) Explore streaming, recording, or live-link options to expand reach without overextending members

**3.5** Explore different staging, set designs, and lighting (e.g., immersive experience) to meet target audience segment needs

**3.6** Establish professional collaborations
