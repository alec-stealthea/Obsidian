You are receiving this email as the primary contact for your operating grant with Calgary Arts Development.

As you may know, we have already started working in advance of the 2027-2030 budget cycle to remind City Council of the wide reach our operating grant clients have in Calgary and beyond. One of the projects we supported last year in advance of the municipal election was a pilot project with Creative Calgary, mapping audience and participant postal codes for a sample set of 21 organizations. It was so successful in showcasing the reach our operating grantees that the mayor included the map as part of his Celebration for the Arts on May 4!

Based on the success of that pilot, we are expanding it this year and hoping to include data from as many of our operating grantees as possible. These maps have become one of our best advocacy tools and demonstrates that residents in every neighbourhood and every ward are participating in programming produced by operating grantees. We will use these postal codes to show Councillors how many arts supporters reside in their ward. These audience maps will complement the map of the locations of arts programming that Councillors receive annually which is comprised of the data you submit in your annual programming form. We want to ensure that City Council knows how many Calgarians are connected to the arts, and how critical these connections are to a thriving city as we ask them prioritize funding and support for arts and culture in the next 4-year budget cycle.

Please see further details on how to participate below, as well as some frequently asked questions. This project is being led by our Research & Policy Manager Gregory Burbidge, copied here. Greg and I will be happy to answer any questions you have about this work.

The deadline to confirm participation and share the data with us is June 2, so we can share our map at our Annual Shareholders Meeting with the City and Report to Community in late June (information about those events will be released soon!)

Invitation to Participate (Optional)

I am writing to invite you to participate in the expansion of this project. Participation is optional, but your participation would be an invaluable contribution to our advocacy working leading into the November budget deliberations.

All we need is a single file with the postal codes of the arts supporters you served in your most recently ended season or fiscal year. This could include:
audiences
attendees
participants
students
volunteers

Postal codes are usually collected through ticketing or registration platforms. Excel or CSV files are the best way to share the data with us.  
 

If you are interested in participating, we will send you a data privacy and protection agreement to ensure that this data does not contain personal information beyond the postal codes and that CADA will not use it for any other purpose. Once we’ve signed the protection agreement you can send along your data file to Greg by email. 

What’s in it for my organization?


In addition to helping support our case for the 2027 - 2030 budget, we’ll also be able to send you a personalized map of just your specific data! Organizations that received the map last year used it to show the reach that they had in Calgary and beyond. If you are interested in any sort of breakdown of your data, we can work with you to identify how it can be broken out based on what is included in your records, for example: individual ticket sales vs subscribers vs volunteers. It’ll take a little time for the personalized maps to be available depending on how many organizations agree to participate, but we’ll be able to complete them through the summer months.

What if my organization doesn’t collect postal code data?

That’s ok! We understand that collecting and retaining postal code data from audiences, attendees and participants can be challenging, especially if your organization does not program ticketed or registered events. We do not expect you to try to go back and gather postal codes if you don’t already have the data stored from the last year. 

If you don’t have postal code data to share, but still want to be part of this work, there’s another way you can help us. We are interested in learning more about the challenges, barriers and opportunities that might exist for arts organizations to collect and maintain audience data as we consider how our ongoing research and data collection practices could be more reciprocal and beneficial to the sector as a whole. We can also be a conversation partner and sounding board if you are interested in starting to collect audience data but don’t know where to start. 

Thank you for considering being part of this work!

If you are interested in participating, just let our Research Manager Gregory Burbidge know at greg.burbidge@calgaryartsdevelopment.com. He can then get you the data sharing agreement and answer any questions you might have.
