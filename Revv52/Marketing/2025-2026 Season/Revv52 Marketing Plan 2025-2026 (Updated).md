# Revv52 Marketing Plan — 2025-2026 Season

*Updated with Audience Segmentation Research Insights (November 2025)*

> [!info] What Changed
> This plan was updated based on the June 2025 audience segmentation research. Key changes:
> - Simplified 7 research segments into 3 actionable audience groups
> - Created a standard messaging template that works for 87% of audience
> - Focused on amplifying what works: email and word-of-mouth (75% of ticket sales)
> - Streamlined campaign execution to respect volunteer capacity

## Introduction

As a non-profit organization, investments in both time and financial resources are limited. This marketing plan shows how the investment of resources will help us achieve the outcomes in the [[Revv52 Draft Strategic Plan 2026-2028|Revv52 Strategic Plan]] while respecting our operational reality as an all-volunteer organization.

The key to any marketing plan is understanding clearly what the goals and objectives are. This plan balances ambition with executability.

## Goals & Objectives

### Financial Measures

- **Bums in Seats:** Sell at least 80% of all seats for the season (budget at 80%, aim for 90%)
- **Eyes on Videos:** YouTube revenue back to at least $500/year
- **Public Donations:** $5,000 in public donations through website

### Strategic Alignment

- **Audience Development & Diversification:** Test new venues, leverage collaborations, expand reach
- **Community Connection:** Deepen engagement with existing fans while making sharing easier
- **Member Participation:** Build sustainable volunteer marketing capacity

## Understanding Our Audience

**Key Finding:** The audience segmentation study (n=431) identified 7 distinct segments. However, with 3,760 email subscribers and limited digital targeting capabilities, we cannot practically execute 7 separate strategies.

**Practical Insight:** Group the audience into 3 actionable categories:

| Audience Group | Research Segments Included | Marketing Approach |
|---|---|---|
| **CORE FANS** (68%) | Art-Goers (37%), Connection-Seekers (14%), Energetics (14%), Community-Engagers (9%) | Keep doing what works. They value entertainment, local arts, and community. Email and word-of-mouth are effective. |
| **CASUALS** (19%) | Highest income, youngest audience. Come when invited or when it fits schedule. | Make sharing easy. Focus on member word-of-mouth tools. These people come through invitations from Core Fans. |
| **OTHERS** (13%) | Values-Prioritizers (4%), For-Mes (3%), Other (6%) | Too small to dedicate volunteer resources. Don't create special campaigns for them. |

## Core Marketing Strategy

### Two-Pronged Approach Based on Research

#### 1. One Strong Message Per Show

Rather than varying messages by segment (which we can't target anyway), create ONE compelling message that speaks to multiple motivations at once.

**Standard Message Template:**

**Subject Line:** [Show Theme] at Revv52 - [Date]

**Opening (1-2 sentences):** Lead with show theme and what makes it special

**Core Message (3-4 sentences hitting these points):**
- World-class local talent (Community-Engagers)
- Entertaining evening out (Energetics, Art-Goers)
- Music that moves you (Connection-Seekers)
- Perfect night to bring friends (Casuals)

**Call to Action:**
- Clear ticket link (with GTM tracking)
- Date, time, venue
- Parking info (research showed this matters)

#### 2. Amplify Word-of-Mouth

> 75% of ticket sales come from email blasts and word-of-mouth. Make sharing easier for existing fans rather than investing in complex digital targeting.

**Practical Amplification Tactics:**
- **Ready-to-Share Social Graphics:** 2-3 simple graphics per show (square format). Send to members 2 weeks before with caption templates.
- **Video Clips:** 15-30 second clips from past shows with ticket links
- **Email Share Button:** Forward-to-friend in MailChimp with pre-populated subject line
- **Member Incentives:** Members who bring 2+ new ticket buyers get early access to next show
- **Collaboration Cross-Promotion:** Co-branded share graphics with collaborators (e.g., Young Canadians)

## 4-6 Week Campaign Calendar

Each show follows this timeline to respect volunteer capacity and optimize the short sales window:

### Week 1 (6 Weeks Out): Assets Creation
- Copywriter: Draft show announcement using message template
- Social Media: Create 2-3 shareable graphics (square format)
- Video: Select and clip 1-2 short videos from past shows

### Week 2 (5 Weeks Out): Launch
- Send announcement email to full Fans list (3,760 subscribers)
- Post to Facebook and Instagram
- Website: Activate show page with ticket links
- Send share kit to all members (graphics + video clips + caption templates)

### Week 3 (4 Weeks Out): Sustaining Donors
- Ken Wilson: Targeted email to sustaining donors
- Social: Repost member shares (amplify word-of-mouth)

### Week 4 (3 Weeks Out): Reminder Wave
- Second email to full Fans list (shorter, urgency-based)
- Social: Post video clips
- Traditional media: Stuart submits to local outlets

### Weeks 5-6 (Final Push): Last Call
- Final email 1 week before show
- Daily social posts in final week
- Last-minute ticket availability updates

## Marketing Channels

### MailChimp (Primary Revenue Driver)

Email drives 75% of ticket sales — this is our most important channel.

**Audience Segments:**
- **Fans** (3,760 subscribers) — Primary list for show announcements and ticket sales
- **Sustaining Donors** (10 active) — Tagged subset managed by Ken Wilson
- **Potential Members** — Audition campaign list with automated sequences
- **Media** — Press contacts for releases and announcements

**Key Tactics:**
- Use message template consistently for all show announcements
- 3 emails per show: launch, reminder, last call
- Add forward-to-friend button to enable word-of-mouth

### Facebook (4,028 followers)

Primary social platform for our demographic (45-80 years old). Post 3 times per week during campaigns.

**Content:** Show announcements, member shares (repost/amplify), video clips, behind-the-scenes

### Instagram (1,547 followers)

Growing 13% year-over-year. Younger demographic overlap with Casuals segment.

**Focus:** Visual storytelling (square graphics), short video clips (15-30 sec), Stories for real-time engagement

### YouTube (3,259 subscribers)

Dual purpose: marketing and revenue generation. Goal: $500+ annual revenue.

**Strategy:** Clear video backlog on schedule; create 30-sec show promo ads; optimize titles/descriptions/thumbnails per [[Revv52 Brand Toolkit|Brand Toolkit]] specs

### Website

Central hub integrating Google Tag Manager for tracking ticket purchase sources.

**Key Pages:**
- Home: Dynamic landing page based on marketing calendar
- Show pages: Live during ticket sales with GTM tracking links
- Audition page: MailChimp signup for potential members
- Donations: Canada Helps and Benevity integration
- Video gallery: Past show content with embedded YouTube

## Measuring Success

Track using existing tools: Google Analytics, MailChimp reports, and ticket sales data.

### Per-Show Metrics
- **Ticket Sales:** % of capacity sold (budget: 80%, target: 90%)
- **Email Performance:** Open rates and click-through rates on ticket links
- **Word-of-Mouth Amplification:** Member sharing activity (post-show survey)
- **Traffic Sources:** GTM data on which channels drove ticket purchases

### Annual Metrics
- **Audience Growth:** Email list growth (10% growth last year — maintain or exceed)
- **Social Media:** Follower growth, especially Instagram (13% growth last year)
- **YouTube Revenue:** Annual revenue vs $500 target
- **Donations:** Online donations vs $5,000 target

### Engagement Metrics
- **Applause Rate:** Approval actions (likes) / Total followers
- **Amplification Rate:** Post shares / Total followers
- **Virality:** Shares / Unique impressions

## What NOT to Do

Based on our operational reality as an all-volunteer organization:

- **Don't segment email list by the 7 research segments** — you can't identify who is in which segment
- **Don't create 7 different messages per show** — volunteers will burn out and messaging will be inconsistent
- **Don't over-invest in Meta/Instagram ads** — small follower base and limited digital effectiveness to date
- **Don't ignore what's working** — 75% from email and word-of-mouth is excellent
- **Don't add complexity requiring professional marketing skills** — keep it simple and repeatable

## Conclusion

This updated plan integrates the June 2025 audience segmentation research while respecting our all-volunteer reality. Rather than complex multi-segment strategies, we focus on:

1. **One strong message that speaks to 87% of our audience**
2. **Amplifying what works: email and word-of-mouth**
3. **Simple, repeatable processes that volunteers can execute consistently**

The research confirmed that 68% of our audience are Core Fans who value entertainment, local arts, and community. Our 75% success rate with email and word-of-mouth shows we're already reaching them effectively. This plan makes that success more systematic and sustainable.
