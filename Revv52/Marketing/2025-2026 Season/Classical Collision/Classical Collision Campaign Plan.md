# Classical Collision — Campaign Plan

**Campaign Name:** *Classical Collision: This Day in Music History*
**Show Dates:** May 7 & 8, 2026 — Mount Royal University, Bella Concert Hall
**Campaign Window:** March 2 – May 8, 2026 (~9 weeks)
**Channels:** Meta (Facebook + Instagram), Mailchimp (Revv52 Fans list)
**Budget:** Organic only (no paid spend)

---

## 1. Campaign Overview

Revv52's Classical Collision fuses classical masterworks with rock legends — and the marketing should feel just as unexpected. The core campaign mechanic is a weekly **"This Day in Music History"** content series that spotlights historical moments tied to artists on the setlist, building curiosity week by week until audiences realize the full picture on show night.

**Primary Objective:** Sell 90%+ of seats for both May 7 & 8 performances.

**Secondary Objectives:**

- Grow the Mailchimp fan list by 15% during the campaign window.
- Increase Instagram engagement rate (likes + comments + shares ÷ followers) by 25% over the previous campaign.
- Generate at least 3 pieces of member-created or user-generated content to amplify organic reach.

## 2. Target Audience

*Based on Revv52's needs-based audience segmentation (Finley & Associates, 2025 Audience Engagement Survey, n=430). See the Revv52 Strategic Plan 2026–2028, Table 5 for the full segmentation model.*

### Audience Profile at a Glance

Revv52's current audience is primarily female, over 65, and white/Caucasian. 86% live in Calgary, 76% are in a relationship, and 30% have at least one dependent. Their top entertainment spending (outside the home) goes to dining out, music concerts, live theatre/travelling shows, and physical activities. A critical behavioural insight: women drive attendance decisions, and there is typically a 48-hour window between the decision to attend and the actual ticket purchase — which means well-timed final-push communications matter enormously.

### Priority Segments for Classical Collision

**1. Art-Goers (37% of audience) — Strategy: GROW**
The largest segment. Mostly women with average income who attend a wide range of arts events (dance, music, theatre, visual art) for enrichment and escape. They value flexible show times and diverse experiences over venue proximity or formality. Critically, they are the *least connected* to Revv52 — meaning they're active arts consumers who simply haven't found us yet or haven't committed. 89% are open to both new-age and traditional shows, which makes Classical Collision's genre-mashup concept a natural fit.

*Campaign implication:* This is the segment to reach with the "This Day in Music History" series on Facebook. The content positions Revv52 as culturally interesting (not just another choir) and gives Art-Goers a reason to pay attention. The classical-meets-rock concept is exactly the kind of diverse, unexpected experience they seek. They need to discover Revv52, see something that piques their curiosity, and have an easy path to tickets. Member sharing is essential — Art-Goers are least connected, so personal recommendations from Revv52 members into their social networks is a primary discovery channel.

**2. Casuals (19% of audience) — Strategy: CONVERT**
The highest-income and youngest segment. They attend if an interesting show comes up that fits their schedule — they aren't seeking out arts events proactively. They have the fewest plans for future shows, making them the hardest to reach but the most valuable to convert (high disposable income, potential for becoming repeat attendees). Less likely to be musicians themselves.

*Campaign implication:* Instagram Reels and the trivia/history hook are designed for this segment. Casuals won't seek out a choir concert, but a Reel about Led Zeppelin or Fleetwood Mac history that happens to mention a live show might catch them mid-scroll. Two show nights (May 7 & 8) gives scheduling flexibility, which matters to this segment. The 48-hour decision window means the final-week Instagram countdown and urgency posts are critical for Casual conversion.

**3. Energetics (14% of audience) — Strategy: CORE (retain & activate)**
Highly connected to Revv52 already — these are the fans who need shows to be entertaining, funny, energetic, and interactive. Average age and income, less likely to be musicians. They attend because Revv52 delivers an experience, not just a performance.

*Campaign implication:* Energetics don't need to be convinced — they need to be informed and activated. Mailchimp is the primary channel. They're also the most likely to share content, so creating shareable moments (dramatic O Fortuna teases, the Handel/Delibes April 14 coincidence) gives them ammunition to recruit their friends (pulling Art-Goers and Casuals into the funnel).

**4. Connection-Seekers (14% of audience) — Strategy: DEEPEN**
They seek entertainment that aligns with their soul and speaks to them in ways other entertainment doesn't. Most likely to be musicians themselves. Average connection to Revv52 and average future show plans.

*Campaign implication:* The depth of the "This Day in Music History" storytelling — not just trivia, but the *why* behind each piece — resonates with Connection-Seekers. The Jade Elephant partnership and the classical-to-rock narrative arc of the show speak to their desire for authentic artistic experiences. Longer-form Facebook captions and the Mailchimp emails (which can carry more story) are the right channels.

**5. Community-Engagers (9%), Values-Prioritizers (4%), For-Mes (3%)**
Smaller segments that will be reached through the broader campaign but don't require segment-specific tactics for this show. Community-Engagers (youngest, most connected, most future shows planned) are natural amplifiers — encourage them to share. Values-Prioritizers and For-Mes (oldest, most connected) are reached through Mailchimp and Facebook.

### Key Barriers

- **Low brand awareness.** Only 49% of survey respondents are connected to Revv52 in some way; 45% are not connected at all. The biggest single barrier is that people don't know Revv52 exists.
- **Perception gap.** The word "choir" carries baggage. Classical Collision's concept (rock band + classical + Jade Elephant) directly counters this, but only if the marketing leads with the *experience* rather than the format.
- **Social media engagement is low-importance to the audience.** Respondents rated social media engagement (28%) as the *least important* aspect of Revv52's offering. This doesn't mean social media is useless — it means we should use it as a *discovery and awareness* tool (reaching Art-Goers and Casuals), not as an engagement-for-engagement's-sake play. Email and word-of-mouth convert; social media introduces.

## 3. Key Messages

**Core Message:** *What happens when Mozart meets the Stones? Classical Collision — where the world's greatest music crashes together on one stage.*

**Supporting Messages (mapped to segments):**

1. **The Unexpected Mashup** — "You've never heard a choir like this. Rock band meets Requiem. Hallelujah Chorus meets funk. This isn't your grandma's concert."
→ *Primary target: Art-Goers and Casuals.* Breaks the "choir = boring" perception barrier. Leads with the experience, not the format — which the audience research confirms is what drives attendance decisions. Art-Goers value diverse experiences; Casuals need a compelling reason to show up.

2. **The Music History Hook** — "Every great song has a story. We're counting down to Classical Collision by reliving the moments that made these songs legendary."
→ *Primary target: Casuals and Connection-Seekers.* This is the "discovery" message — it doesn't sell the show directly but builds curiosity through content that has standalone value. Casuals encounter it on Instagram; Connection-Seekers appreciate the depth on Facebook and email.

3. **The Live Experience** — "52 voices. Individual mics. A full rock band. Jade Elephant. Two nights only at the Bella Concert Hall."
→ *Primary target: Art-Goers and Energetics.* The audience survey rated music selection (94%), staging/lighting (85%), and value for money (84%) as the top three most important aspects of Revv52's offering. This message hits all three. Two nights also signals scheduling flexibility — important for Art-Goers who are busy and need options.

4. **The Community Invite** — "Bring someone who loves music. They'll thank you after."
→ *Primary target: Energetics (to activate sharing) and Community-Engagers.* 76% of the audience is in a relationship; attending events is a social act. Energetics are already sold — this gives them a word-of-mouth prompt to bring Art-Goers and Casuals who haven't discovered Revv52 yet. The 48-hour decision window means personal recommendations are the most powerful conversion tool in the final weeks.

## 4. Channel Strategy

### Meta — Facebook
**Primary segments served:** Art-Goers (discovery), Connection-Seekers (depth), Energetics (sharing)

**Why:** Facebook is the primary discovery channel for Art-Goers (the largest segment at 37%) and the storytelling channel for Connection-Seekers. The audience skews female, 65+, Calgary-based — this is a Facebook-first demographic. Event pages drive organic reach through RSVPs and shares, and critically, every "Interested" click becomes visible to that person's network — which is how Art-Goers (the least-connected segment) discover Revv52.

**Content approach:**
- Facebook Event page (both nights) as the anchor — all content links back here.
- Weekly "This Day in Music History" posts with **longer captions** that tell a story (Connection-Seekers want depth; Art-Goers want something interesting enough to stop scrolling).
- Show countdown posts in the final 2 weeks, timed to the 48-hour decision window.
- Cross-posts with Jade Elephant and MRU pages where possible — these extend reach into networks Revv52 doesn't currently touch.
- Actively encourage Energetics and Community-Engagers (the most connected segments) to share posts to their personal profiles.

**Effort:** Medium (1–2 posts/week plus event management).

### Meta — Instagram
**Primary segments served:** Casuals (discovery), Community-Engagers (amplification)

**Why:** Instagram — specifically Reels — is the best organic channel for reaching Casuals, the youngest and highest-income segment. Casuals don't seek out arts events; they encounter them. A Reel about Led Zeppelin or Fleetwood Mac history that *happens* to mention a live show is exactly the kind of content that can catch a Casual mid-scroll. Community-Engagers (youngest, most connected) are also active here and serve as natural amplifiers.

**Content approach:**
- Weekly "This Day in Music History" Reels (15–30 seconds — members sharing a fact, humming a tune, or reacting to a music history moment).
- Stories for behind-the-scenes rehearsal content, polls ("Which rock classic are you most excited to hear?"), and countdown stickers.
- Carousel posts for setlist teasers closer to the show.
- Note: the audience research shows social media engagement is rated lowest in importance (28%) — so don't optimize for engagement metrics. Optimize for *reach* and *discovery*. One Casual who buys two $50 tickets is worth more than 100 likes from existing followers.

**Effort:** Medium-High (1–2 Reels/week plus Stories).

### Mailchimp — Revv52 Fans
**Primary segments served:** Energetics (conversion), Connection-Seekers (storytelling), Values-Prioritizers & For-Mes (retention)

**Why:** The most direct line to people who already care about Revv52. Email converts to ticket sales better than social media — and the audience research confirms this: respondents rated email communications as highly important, well above social media. The Energetics and Connection-Seekers on this list are the most likely to buy early. The 48-hour decision window means the final email (1 week out) needs to be perfectly timed and urgency-driven.

**Content approach:**
- **4 campaign emails** timed to key moments (see calendar below).
- Each email features a "This Day in Music History" highlight and a clear ticket CTA.
- Final email is an urgency play ("Last chance — limited seats remain").

**Effort:** Low-Medium (1 email every ~2 weeks).

## 9. Risks and Mitigations

**Risk 1: Content creation bottleneck.** The weekly cadence requires consistent production from volunteer committee members who have other commitments.
→ *Mitigation:* Batch-produce the static graphics 2–3 weeks ahead using the template. Keep Reels simple (phone-quality is fine — authenticity beats polish). Assign specific members to specific weeks.

**Risk 2: Organic reach limitations.** Without paid spend, Meta's algorithm may limit how many people see each post, especially on Facebook. The audience research confirms 45% of the audience is not connected to Revv52 at all — organic social alone won't reach them.
→ *Mitigation:* Lean on Reels (Instagram's algorithm favours short video). Activate Energetics and Community-Engagers (the most connected segments) to share posts to their personal profiles — 52 members × their networks is the primary organic reach multiplier for the unconnected Art-Goer segment. Use the Mailchimp list as the reliable conversion channel. Cross-post with Jade Elephant and MRU pages to tap into adjacent audiences.

**Risk 3: History-to-setlist tie-ins feel forced in some weeks.** Not every week has a perfect calendar match.
→ *Mitigation:* Week 6 (Fleetwood Mac) and Week 8 (Led Zeppelin) use "spring of that year" framing rather than exact dates — this still works editorially. Week 9 drops the history format entirely and pivots to direct show promotion, which is the right call for the final push.
